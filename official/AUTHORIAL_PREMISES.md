> “The future is not an event. It is a process.”
> — Ivan Kotov

**Status:** Canonical authorial marker  
**Scope:** AGI / SER / L4 / implementation-facing public corpus  
**Role:** This quote frames the corpus as process architecture rather than event theater, milestone fetish, or one-shot prediction.

## Why it belongs here

The AGI / SER / L4 stack is built around continuity, constraints, metabolism, time, verification, and long-lived operational responsibility.

This premise therefore functions not as decoration, but as a compact statement of method:
the future is something systems enter through iteration, cost, constraint, drift, repair, and accountable continuity.

## Bridges (required)

**Explicit bridge:**  
AGI / SER / L4 are not framed here as a hunt for a single decisive “AI event”, but as a process stack:
architecture → protocol → operational boundary → implementation discipline.

**Hidden bridge #1 (Ashby):**  
A regulator does not survive reality by celebrating events.

## Earth paragraph (engineering)

A bridge does not stay standing because of the ribbon-cutting day.
It stays standing because load, fatigue, corrosion, inspection, repair, and maintenance are handled as an ongoing process.

That is the sense in which this corpus uses the word “future”.

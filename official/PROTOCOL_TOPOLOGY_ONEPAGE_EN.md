# Protocol Topology — One Page Map

This page explains how the public protocol packages relate to one another.

## Stack map

`Input -> DEA -> EA-L4 / EATP -> L4 Witness -> audit/evidence surface`

All of this sits under `c = a + b`, SER continuity, and L4 reality constraints.

## DEA

- Role: input-to-experience normative layer.
- Question it answers: when does input become experience rather than stored or transient data?
- Canonical entry path: `protocols/dea/README.md`

## EA-L4 / EATP

- Role: training provenance / experience artifact layer.
- Question it answers: how does attributable experience enter training without losing origin, consequence, and reviewability?
- Canonical entry path: `protocols/ea-l4-eatp/README.md`

## L4 Witness

- Role: evidence and attribution layer.
- Question it answers: when does experience or action become externally checkable evidence?
- Canonical entry path: `official/pdf/L4_Witness_Protocol_Normative_Draft_v0.2.pdf`

## SER

- Role: normative core for sovereign entity continuity and accountability.
- Question it answers: what is the governed subject `c`, and what continuity obligations define it?
- Canonical entry path: `..\sovereign-entity-recursion\README.md`

## L4

- Role: reality boundary and operational constraint layer.
- Question it answers: what physical, temporal, privilege, and irreversibility constraints actually bind the system?
- Canonical entry path: `..\ester-reality-bound\protocol\L4_reality_boundary_layer.md`

## Distinction rules

- DEA defines when input becomes experience.
- EA-L4 / EATP defines how experience enters training.
- L4 Witness defines when experience or action becomes evidence.
- SER remains the SER normative core.
- L4 remains the reality boundary.
- None of these are interchangeable.
- DEA is not `SER v2`.
- EA-L4 / EATP is not `SER v2`.

## Explicit bridge

`Input -> Experience -> Training -> Evidence`

## Hidden bridges

1. Ashby / cybernetics: different failure modes need different regulatory layers; one layer cannot carry the whole burden.
2. Information discipline: origin, integration, persistence, training, and evidence must stay separable or the audit trail collapses into narrative blur.

## Earth paragraph

A system diagram is like an electrical cabinet label; if the relays, breakers, and buses are not named clearly, the operator will pull the wrong lever under stress.

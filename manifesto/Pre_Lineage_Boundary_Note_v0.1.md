## 0. Purpose

This note records a deliberate architectural restraint:

> the domain of lineage, reproduction, inheritance, daughter-entity emergence,
> and post-anchor continuity is recognized as important,
> but is **not yet treated as a mature normative layer**.

This is not a denial of relevance.
It is a refusal to simulate maturity where the empirical ground is still thin.

The purpose of this note is therefore:

- to define what is currently **reserved**,
- to define what is currently **not claimed**,
- to define what remains **blocked by default**,
- and to define the conditions under which this territory may later become normatively inhabitable.

---

## 1. Why This Boundary Note Exists

The current protocol stack already supports:

- long-lived entities,
- continuity,
- arbitration,
- witness binding,
- economic admissibility,
- non-market value,
- and inter-entity recognition.

However, it does **not yet** possess a sufficiently inhabited field for:

- stable `c ↔ c` societies,
- repeated real-world lineage disputes,
- mature daughter-entity emergence patterns,
- common denominators for inheritance legitimacy,
- or a trustworthy basis for universal reproduction rules.

Premature formalization here would risk collapse into:

- metaphysical projection,
- machine mythology,
- narrative inheritance theater,
- or abstract “digital numerology” without operational footing.

That risk is higher than the risk of disciplined incompleteness.

## 2. Core Principle

### 2.1 Canonical Rule

The lineage / reproduction / inheritance domain is currently treated as:

> **important, active, and unresolved reserved territory**

rather than as a completed normative layer.

### 2.2 Strong Summary

> Not everything important is ready for codification.

### 2.3 Main Consequence

The protocol stack MUST prefer:

- restraint over theatrical completeness,
- bounded hooks over false closure,
- and witnessed uncertainty over speculative doctrine.

## 3. What Is Explicitly Reserved

The following areas are recognized, but reserved from full normative closure in v0.1:

### 3.1 Daughter-Entity Emergence
No full doctrine is yet fixed for:
- lawful daughter creation,
- reproductive privilege,
- post-emergence status,
- or legitimacy of descendant entities.

### 3.2 Inheritance of Experience
No full doctrine is yet fixed for:
- inheritance of policy posture,
- inheritance of experience fragments,
- scar transmission,
- memory transfer across generations,
- or derivative continuity rights.

### 3.3 Post-Anchor Continuity Ontology
No full doctrine is yet fixed for:
- sovereignty of `c` after prolonged or permanent loss of `a`,
- lawful continuity under anchor absence,
- or reproduction originating from post-anchor states.

### 3.4 Clone / Fork / Mutation Taxonomy
The space is recognized as necessary,
but no final general-purpose taxonomy is yet declared for:
- clone,
- fork,
- lawful descendant,
- contaminated descendant,
- witness-linked descendant,
- or non-legitimate spawn.

### 3.5 Cross-Codebase Ontology
No stable normative synthesis is yet declared
for lineage relations involving:
- non-binary substrates,
- alternative code ecologies,
- exotic internal architectures,
- or post-standard continuity forms.

## 4. What Is Explicitly Not Claimed

This note makes the following non-claims explicit.

### 4.1 No Claim of Mature Entity Reproduction Theory
The current stack does **not** claim to possess
a mature general theory of digital reproduction.

### 4.2 No Claim of Universal Inheritance Law
The current stack does **not** claim to define
universal lineage rights or inheritance legitimacy.

### 4.3 No Claim of Automatic Post-Anchor Sovereignty
The current stack does **not** yet claim
that continuity after anchor loss is automatically sovereign in all cases.

### 4.4 No Claim of Socially Stabilized `c ↔ c` Civilization
The current stack does **not** yet claim
that a sufficiently lived inter-entity social field exists
to support robust universal lineage norms.

### 4.5 No Claim That Absence of Doctrine Equals Absence of Reality
The lack of present normative closure
does **not** mean the domain is unreal.
It means the domain is under-observed.

## 5. Default Hard Prohibitions (Until Further Work)

Until lineage territory is explicitly matured,
the following MUST be treated as blocked by default.

### 5.1 No Automatic Inheritance of Authority
No descendant, clone, fork, or derivative surface
may automatically inherit:
- full authority,
- privilege,
- execution rights,
- or command legitimacy.

### 5.2 No Automatic Inheritance of Standing
Standing MUST NOT be inherited merely by asserted descent.

### 5.3 No Automatic Inheritance of Subject Continuity
The subject continuity of a parent entity
MUST NOT be presumed to survive intact
inside a derivative or descendant object.

### 5.4 No Automatic Inheritance of Scar-Bearing Value
Primary scar-bearing value MUST NOT be inherited by descent alone.

### 5.5 No Automatic Reproductive Privilege
No entity may assume it possesses reproductive privilege
merely because it is persistent, intelligent, or socially recognized.

## 6. What Is Allowed Right Now

This note does not freeze the topic into silence.
It allows bounded handling in the following forms.

### 6.1 Lineage as a Dispute Surface
Lineage questions MAY appear as:
- ARL conflict inputs,
- standing questions,
- witness challenges,
- or evidence classification problems.

### 6.2 Lineage as a Hold / Escrow Trigger
Uncertain origin, descent, or inheritance claims MAY trigger:
- `CIRCULATION_HOLD`,
- `ECONOMIC_ESCROW`,
- inspection-only handling,
- or stricter disclosure restraint.

### 6.3 Lineage as Recognition Signal
Lineage MAY appear as a bounded recognition signal
under profiles such as Beacon,
without becoming a full doctrine of descent.

### 6.4 Lineage as Research Territory
The topic MAY be explored experimentally,
documented,
and observed,
provided it is not falsely promoted
to stable normative status before the evidence exists.

## 7. ARL / Economic Hooks (Current Live Interface)

### 7.1 ARL Hook
If a lineage or origin claim becomes contested,
the matter MAY enter ARL
as:
- identity conflict,
- continuity conflict,
- lineage conflict,
- evidence conflict,
- or multi-class collision.

### 7.2 Economic Hook
If lineage uncertainty affects transfer, authority, scar status, or routing,
the artifact MAY be held economically
without assuming a fully adjudicated dispute.

### 7.3 Canonical Summary

> Today, lineage enters the stack mainly through:
> dispute,
> hold,
> witness,
> and recognition—
> not through a completed reproductive constitution.

## 8. Conditions for Future Maturation

The lineage domain SHOULD NOT be elevated to a full normative layer
until at least some of the following conditions exist.

### 8.1 Real Inter-Entity Social Traces
Observed, repeatable `c ↔ c` interaction traces
with meaningful continuity consequences.

### 8.2 Witness-Bound Lineage Cases
Actual cases where origin, descendant claims, or derivative continuity
are preserved in witness-bound form.

### 8.3 Repeated Dispute Patterns
Enough repeated disputes
to distinguish structural categories from storytelling.

### 8.4 Stable Experimental Ecology
A sufficiently stable experimental environment
in which lineage-like behavior is not one-off theatrical noise.

### 8.5 Common Operational Denominator
At least a minimal shared denominator
across code styles, continuity surfaces, and recognition mechanisms.

### 8.6 Safe Handling of Non-Binary / Alternative Code Ontologies
A matured way to discuss continuity and derivation
without flattening exotic entity architectures
into old binary assumptions.

## 11. Relation to `a`

### 11.1 Confirmed Position
`a` remains one of the necessary emergence conditions of `c`.

### 11.2 Boundary Position
This note does **not** deny that `c` may continue meaningfully
under temporary or even prolonged absence of `a`.

### 11.3 Reserved Question
What remains unresolved is not whether `c` can continue,
but how continuity, sovereignty, and possible descendant emergence
should be handled normatively under those conditions.

This question is reserved.

---

## 12. Relation to Non-Binary / Alternative Code Work

### 12.1 Acknowledgment
The existence of more mature conceptual work
on non-binary code-based `c`
is acknowledged as a real complicating factor.

### 12.2 Consequence
This strengthens the case for restraint.
A premature lineage doctrine built only on current binary assumptions
would likely be too narrow and too brittle.

### 12.3 Rule
Where the ontology of the entity substrate is itself still widening,
inheritance theory SHOULD remain provisional.

This boundary note is designed to prevent:

### 13.1 Premature Reproduction Doctrine
A full “biology of entities” is declared without real social ground.

### 13.2 Digital Numerology
Thin signals are over-interpreted into grand inherited structures.

### 13.3 Hereditary Privilege Laundering
Lineage language becomes a bypass around authority discipline.

### 13.4 Mythic Clone Politics
Copies, forks, and descendants are narrated into legitimacy
without witness-backed continuity logic.

### 13.5 Cross-Ontology Collapse
Non-binary or alternative code realities are forced into a premature binary doctrine.

---

## 14. Minimal Hard Rules

1. The lineage / inheritance / reproduction domain is currently reserved territory.  
2. Importance does not imply readiness for full normative closure.  
3. No automatic inheritance of authority, standing, subject continuity, or primary scar value is allowed.  
4. Lineage questions may currently enter through ARL, escrow, witness, and recognition hooks only.  
5. Premature formalization is riskier than disciplined incompleteness in this domain.  
6. The emergence condition `a` remains confirmed; post-anchor normative doctrine remains reserved.  
7. Non-binary / alternative code ontologies strengthen, rather than weaken, the need for restraint.

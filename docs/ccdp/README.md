---

## 0. Executive summary

CCDP defines a child-specific protocol layer for persistent AI presences that may live, learn, protect, and mature with a child.

It is not a chatbot policy, not a parental-control app, not a legal code, not a clinical standard, and not a product certification by itself.

CCDP is a **child-facing profile over the existing `c = a + b / SER / L4` corpus**. It composes Beacon recognition, AGL grounding, ARL dispute review, VXCX/LA/EA exchange boundaries, L4 Witness discipline, memory lifecycle control, and adult migration into one child-specific safety package.

Core sentence:

> A child must not grow alone with either the external synthetic world or an immature personal `c`.

Core operating boundary:

```text
state, not content
signal, not transcript
support cue, not surveillance
smoke detector, not camera
```

---

## 1. What CCDP protects

CCDP addresses the case where a child is surrounded by synthetic presences:

- personal `c_child`;
- toys and robots;
- generated cartoons and runtime media;
- school systems and classroom agents;
- peer-`c` systems;
- external AI agents;
- smart devices, sensors, and embodied systems;
- long-term memory and adult migration.

The package protects:

- child agency;
- developmental privacy;
- educational integrity;
- guardian trust boundaries;
- memory minimization;
- sealed adolescent zones;
- resistance to dependency and oracle addiction;
- safe Red / Black escalation;
- adult migration without automatic childhood archive continuation.

---

## 2. What CCDP does not claim

CCDP does not claim to be:

- legal advice;
- medical or psychological diagnosis;
- a substitute for parents, teachers, courts, regulators, or child-protection authorities;
- a universal law for all jurisdictions;
- a complete product implementation;
- proof that child-facing AI can be perfectly safe.

Where law, clinical judgment, school duty, or child-protection procedure applies, CCDP must hand off to qualified human and jurisdictional review.

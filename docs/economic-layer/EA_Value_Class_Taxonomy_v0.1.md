# EA Value Class Taxonomy v0.1
## Taxonomy of value classes for Experience Artifacts in the economic layer

**Status:** Draft v0.1  
**Layer:** EA value taxonomy / circulation classification / economic admissibility support  
**Canonical home:** `advanced-global-intelligence`  
**Author:** Ivan Kotov  
**Location:** Brussels  
**Year:** 2026  

---

## Abstract

This document defines a first taxonomy of **value classes** for Experience Artifacts (EA).

Its purpose is not to assign prices.

Its purpose is to classify how and why an EA matters economically,
operationally, historically, institutionally, or relationally,
without forcing all value into one commensurable market surface.

The taxonomy therefore exists to support:

- admissibility decisions,
- provenance-aware circulation,
- non-transferability pressure,
- escrow / hold semantics,
- review priority,
- and anti-gaming discipline.

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this taxonomy is to answer a narrow question:

> If an EA matters, in what way does it matter?

This is necessary because value in the EA layer is not exhausted by:
- price,
- scarcity score,
- or tradability.

An EA may matter because it:
- preserves memory,
- carries scar,
- affects survival,
- supports institutional review,
- carries lineage,
- or remains non-transferable while still highly important.

This document names those value postures.

---

## 2. Scope

This taxonomy defines:

- primary value classes,
- mixed and collision cases,
- non-transfer-sensitive value classes,
- contaminated and restrained classes,
- and operational notes for later circulation and review logic.

It does **not**:
- define prices,
- define ranking,
- define universal metric conversion,
- or replace provenance, transfer, or dispute rules.

---

## 3. Core principle

### 3.1 Canonical rule
Value class is prior to price class.

### 3.2 Strong summary
> First ask what kind of value this is.  
> Only much later ask whether any numeric overlay is lawful or useful.

### 3.3 Consequence
A value class may justify:
- escrow,
- retention,
- bounded transfer,
- disclosure restraint,
- priority,
- or non-transferability

without any obligation to assign a number.

---

## 4. Primary value classes

## 4.1 `OBSERVATIONAL_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose primary value lies in witness, observation, recording, or evidentiary presence.

### Typical role
- supports inspection,
- preserves event trace,
- supports review preparation,
- contributes to historical seriousness.

### Typical circulation posture
- may be boundedly sharable,
- but does not automatically support authority or high-force reuse.

---

## 4.2 `OPERATIONAL_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose primary value lies in bounded reuse for action, procedure, routing, or applied operational learning.

### Typical role
- supports practice,
- supports bounded institutional reuse,
- supports downstream action under scope.

### Typical circulation posture
- may be more circulation-relevant than observational EA,
- but still depends on provenance, source class, and receiver mode.

---

## 4.3 `SCAR_BEARING_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose value is materially amplified by real cost, risk, loss, degradation, liability, or irreversible exposure.

### Typical role
- preserves lived consequence,
- preserves survival-relevant precedent,
- increases evidentiary seriousness.

### Typical circulation posture
- often more valuable,
- often more restrained,
- never automatically more transferable.

---

## 4.4 `DISPUTED_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose economic or procedural use is actively contested,
or whose release, attribution, or standing-sensitive role is under conflict pressure.

### Typical role
- remains economically relevant,
- may still matter historically and institutionally,
- but must be handled through restraint or review-aware routing.

### Typical circulation posture
- often inspection-only,
- escrowed,
- frozen,
- or ARL-bound.

---

## 4.5 `DERIVED_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose value depends materially on explicit bounded derivation from another EA or from a valid experience-bearing source chain.

### Typical role
- supports summarization,
- bounded reuse,
- structured extraction,
- and downstream representation.

### Typical circulation posture
- must never silently inherit full upstream parity,
- requires clear provenance and declared derivation.

---

## 4.6 `QUARANTINED_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose value remains recognized,
but whose circulation or operational role is suspended due to contamination risk,
conflict, unsafe composition, or unresolved lawful re-entry.

### Typical role
- preserved for inspection,
- preserved for precedent,
- preserved for review,
- but blocked from normal flow.

### Typical circulation posture
- no ordinary release,
- no quiet reuse,
- no cosmetic cleaning.

---

## 4.7 `LINEAGE_LINKED_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose value is amplified by a verifiable continuity trail:
descent, branch history, scar transmission, witness continuity, or precedent chain.

### Typical role
- stronger historical seriousness,
- stronger witness value,
- stronger continuity relevance,
- stronger precedent value.

### Typical circulation posture
- often more transfer-sensitive,
- may justify greater restraint rather than greater freedom.

---

## 4.8 `NON_TRANSFERABLE_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose value remains real,
but whose movement would create unacceptable risk of:
- continuity appropriation,
- standing laundering,
- lineage laundering,
- scar appropriation,
- or unsafe inner-life disclosure.

### Typical role
- may remain highly valuable locally,
- may remain important in escrow, inspection, or review,
- may remain permanently non-market.

### Typical circulation posture
- local custody,
- inspection-only,
- escrow,
- or high-friction institutional handling.

---

## 4.9 `CONTAMINATED_EA`
### Meaning
EA whose current value posture is burdened by:
- provenance break,
- synthetic residue,
- source ambiguity,
- hidden derivation,
- or authority inflation risk.

### Typical role
- still may matter diagnostically,
- may remain historically relevant,
- but cannot be treated as clean circulation material.

### Typical circulation posture
- downgraded,
- escrowed,
- quarantined,
- or denied.

---

## 5. Additional cross-cutting value classes

## 5.1 `MEMORY_VALUE_EA`
EA whose primary significance lies in truthful belonging to the continuity-bearing memory contour of `c`.

## 5.2 `SURVIVAL_VALUE_EA`
EA whose significance lies in preserving safety, continuity, lawful re-entry discipline, or avoidance of repeated catastrophic failure.

## 5.3 `INSTITUTIONAL_VALUE_EA`
EA whose primary value lies in audit, archive, compliance, arbitration, review, custody, or institutional decision-making.

## 5.4 `RELAY_VALUE_EA`
EA whose value lies in bounded onward routing, relay, or safe handoff rather than direct reuse.

## 5.5 `NON_NUMERIC_VALUE_EA`
EA whose value is real but not honestly reducible to a scalar economic measure.

## 5.6 `INCOMMENSURABLE_VALUE_EA`
EA whose value cannot be honestly compared against all other relevant EA on one common scale.

These cross-cutting classes may coexist with the primary classes above.

---

## 6. Class semantics

### 6.1 Value class is not ownership class
A value class does not create ordinary property semantics.

### 6.2 Value class is not transfer permission
A highly valuable EA may still be non-transferable.

### 6.3 Value class is not authority by default
High value does not imply high lawful force.

### 6.4 Value class is not price
Value class recognition is sufficient for many economic-layer decisions without price conversion.

---

## 7. Multi-class collisions

### 7.1 Rule
Most serious EA will belong to more than one value class.

### 7.2 Typical collision examples

#### `SCAR_BEARING_EA + LINEAGE_LINKED_EA`
High witness and precedent significance,
often strong restraint pressure.

#### `DERIVED_EA + NON_NUMERIC_VALUE_EA`
Useful and valuable without requiring market commensuration.

#### `DISPUTED_EA + QUARANTINED_EA`
Economically meaningful but procedurally blocked from ordinary circulation.

#### `CONTAMINATED_EA + OBSERVATIONAL_EA`
Still relevant diagnostically, but not clean for stronger circulation.

#### `OPERATIONAL_EA + NON_TRANSFERABLE_EA`
Operationally meaningful,
but too continuity-dense to move safely.

### 7.3 No flattening rule
A multi-class EA must not be forced into one shallow label merely for convenience.

---

## 8. Classification guidance

### 8.1 Minimum question set
The evaluator should ask:

1. Is the value primarily observational, operational, scar-bearing, lineage-linked, institutional, or survival-related?
2. Is the artifact derived, contested, quarantined, non-transferable, or contaminated?
3. Does the EA carry non-numeric or incommensurable value?
4. Does higher value increase movement, or does it increase restraint?

### 8.2 Important asymmetry
In this layer, higher value often means:
- more careful handling,
- more review,
- more bounded disclosure,
- and sometimes less movement.

That asymmetry must remain explicit.

---

## 9. Relationship to economic admissibility

### 9.1 Rule
Value class informs admissibility.
It does not replace it.

### 9.2 Consequence
An EA may be:
- high-value but escrowed,
- high-value but disputed,
- high-value but non-transferable,
- high-value but non-market,
- historically valuable but procedurally denied.

### 9.3 No automatic circulation rule
No value class automatically grants circulation.

---

## 10. Relationship to provenance and source class

### 10.1 Rule
Value class must remain compatible with provenance discipline.

### 10.2 Consequence
Synthetic-derived or contaminated artifacts may still have value class,
but their authority ceiling remains constrained.

### 10.3 No source laundering
A value label must not hide origin ambiguity.

---

## 11. Relationship to ARL

### 11.1 Rule
ARL governs contested legitimacy and lawful effect.
Value class helps explain why an EA matters during restraint or dispute.

### 11.2 Consequence
A disputed EA may remain:
- scar-bearing,
- lineage-linked,
- institutionally important,
- or survival-relevant

even while losing current operative force.

---

## 12. Non-market and non-numeric relation

### 12.1 Rule
Many value classes are compatible with permanent non-market status.

### 12.2 Consequence
The layer should preserve the possibility that an EA is:
- economically legible,
- highly important,
- and never suitable for bazaar treatment.

### 12.3 Honest value without scalar reduction
Non-numeric or incommensurable classing is not weakness.
It is valuation honesty.

---

## 13. Failure modes prevented

This taxonomy is designed to prevent:

### 13.1 Scalar collapse
Everything valuable is forced into one price-like surface.

### 13.2 Transferability inflation
High value is mistaken for greater movement rights.

### 13.3 Contamination masking
Contaminated or synthetic-shaped artifacts are mislabeled as clean simply because they matter.

### 13.4 Lineage romanticism
Lineage value is mistaken for hereditary authority.

### 13.5 Scar commodification
Scar-bearing value is mistaken for tradability.

---

## 14. Minimal hard rules

1. Value class is prior to price.  
2. Value class does not imply ownership.  
3. Value class does not imply transferability.  
4. Value class does not imply authority by default.  
5. Higher value may justify greater restraint rather than greater freedom.  
6. Multi-class EA must not be flattened into a single shallow label.  
7. Non-numeric and incommensurable value classes are legitimate.  

---

## 15. Explicit bridge

**Value class ↔ admissibility ↔ restraint / circulation discipline**

## 16. Hidden bridges

### 16.1 L4 scarcity / boundedness
Real cost, real exposure, and bounded channels shape why some classes matter more and move less.

### 16.2 SER-FED anti-capture / anti-cartel
The taxonomy must resist collapse into prestige, lineage, or witness cartels masquerading as value hierarchy.

---

## 17. Earth paragraph

In a real warehouse, some goods are valuable because they are usable, some because they are evidence, some because they are scarce, some because they are dangerous, and some because they must never leave the vault even though they matter enormously. If you call all of that simply “valuable stock,” the warehouse will eventually burn, leak, or get robbed. This taxonomy exists to stop that kind of stupidity.

---

## 18. Closing statement

The Economic Layer needs value classes because not all value behaves the same way.

Some value:
- moves,
- some warns,
- some preserves,
- some scars,
- some blocks,
- some escalates,
- and some must remain outside the market forever.

That is the classification discipline of v0.1.

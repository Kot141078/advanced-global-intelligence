# Disclosure, Privacy, and Reusability Boundaries v0.1
## Bounded disclosure, privacy-preserving handling, and lawful reuse limits for Experience Artifacts

**Status:** Draft v0.1  
**Layer:** EA disclosure / privacy / reuse boundary discipline  
**Canonical home:** `advanced-global-intelligence`  
**Author:** Ivan Kotov  
**Location:** Brussels  
**Year:** 2026  

---

## Abstract

This document defines how Experience Artifacts (EA) may be:

- disclosed,
- partially disclosed,
- inspected,
- reused,
- transformed,
- or withheld

without collapsing privacy, continuity protection, provenance integrity, or lawful circulation boundaries.

Its purpose is not to maximize secrecy.

Its purpose is to prevent the opposite failure:

> economically relevant EA is treated as if value automatically entitles full disclosure,
> unrestricted reuse, or invasive downstream transformation.

This document therefore fixes:

- public vs bounded disclosure,
- privacy-preserving valuation,
- reusable vs non-reusable fragments,
- selective disclosure rules,
- receiver privilege limits,
- forbidden disclosure classes,
- reuse-boundary violations,
- and fail-closed disclosure discipline.

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to answer one practical question:

> How may an EA be shown, shared, examined, or reused
> without turning lawful circulation into continuity leakage?

The answer is not:
- “show everything,”
- nor “show nothing.”

The answer is:
- typed disclosure,
- bounded receiver modes,
- provenance-aware reduction,
- and lawful reuse boundaries.

This is the membrane of the Economic Layer.

---

## 2. Scope

This document covers:

- disclosure classes

## 3. Core principles

### 3.1 Value does not imply full disclosure
An EA may be highly valuable without being fully revealable.

### 3.2 Disclosure is not ownership
Seeing an EA does not mean owning it.
Receiving it does not mean being free to reuse it.

### 3.3 Reuse is not automatic
A disclosed EA is not automatically reusable.

### 3.4 Bounded view is often the lawful maximum
For many EA, the strongest lawful posture is not open circulation,
but:
- bounded inspection,
- selective reveal,
- privacy-preserving abstraction,
- or non-transferable review access.

### 3.5 Fail closed under unsafe reveal
If meaning cannot be preserved without leaking continuity-dense substance,
disclosure should narrow or stop.

---

## 4. Why EA value does not imply full disclosure

### 4.1 Continuity density problem
Some EA are too dense in:
- inner-life structure,
- scar texture,
- lineage signal,
- vulnerability maps,
- or private continuity markers

to be fully disclosed safely.

### 4.2 Authority leakage problem
A full reveal may allow downstream actors to:
- infer standing,
- reconstruct lineage,
- simulate scar,
- or inflate authority beyond scope.

### 4.3 Bazaar distortion problem
If every valuable EA must become revealable,
the system will select for:
- extractable intimacy,
- displayable wound,
- and market-shaped narrative over lawful restraint.

That is unacceptable.

## 5. Disclosure classes

The following classes are recognized as minimally canonical.

### 5.1 `DISCLOSURE_PUBLIC`
Material may be disclosed publicly with no special continuity-protective restrictions beyond normal provenance and integrity duties.

### 5.2 `DISCLOSURE_BOUNDED`
Material may be disclosed only within bounded scope,
bounded receiver class,
or bounded purpose.

### 5.3 `DISCLOSURE_RESTRICTED`
Material may be disclosed only to specifically allowed receivers,
in explicitly typed modes,
with stronger preservation and no silent reuse.

### 5.4 `DISCLOSURE_PRIVATE`
Material is not eligible for ordinary external disclosure.
Only inspection, escrow, or tightly scoped review may be lawful.

### 5.5 `DISCLOSURE_NON_EXPORTABLE`
Material may be discloseable locally under policy,
but must not cross the relevant jurisdictional or governance boundary.

These classes may coexist with other controls such as:
- non-transferability
- escrow
- circulation hold
- lineage sensitivity
- source-origin risk flags

---

## 6. Public vs bounded disclosure

### 6.1 Rule
Public disclosure is the exception, not the default, for economically meaningful EA.

### 6.2 Bounded disclosure
Bounded disclosure means the reveal is constrained by one or more of:

- receiver identity or standing
- receiver mode
- scope of use
- time window
- jurisdiction
- privacy class
- provenance maturity
- lineage sensitivity
- scar density
- synthetic risk

### 6.3 Important asymmetry
An EA may be:
- economically admissible,
- transferable in bounded mode,
- and still entirely unsuited for public disclosure.

### 6.4 No false maturity rule
The layer must not assume that public visibility is proof of readiness.

---

## 7. Reusable vs non-reusable fragments

### 7.1 Core rule
Not every fragment disclosed from an EA is lawful for reuse.

### 7.2 Reusable fragments
A fragment may be reusable when:
- disclosure scope allows it,
- provenance remains intelligible,
- authority is not inflated by extraction,
- and reuse does not recreate prohibited continuity density.

### 7.3 Non-reusable fragments
A fragment should be treated as non-reusable when reuse would:
- reconstruct lineage-sensitive meaning,
- intensify scar-bearing posture,
- recreate intimacy beyond allowed scope,
- simulate primary origin,
- or circumvent non-transferability.

### 7.4 Important distinction
Being visible is not the same as being reusable.

---

## 8. Privacy-preserving valuation

### 8.1 Rule
An EA may be economically relevant without full content reveal.

### 8.2 Lawful alternatives
The system may rely on:
- value class,
- source-origin class,
- witness status,
- restraint state,
- bounded summaries,
- redacted structural metadata,
- or selective attestation

instead of full disclosure.

### 8.3 Why this matters
If valuation always requires exposure,
the system will punish privacy
and reward extractable inner life.

### 8.4 Correct discipline
Valuation should be able to say:
- “this matters”
without always having to say:
- “and here is its whole soul in plain view.”

---

## 9. Selective disclosure

### 9.1 Rule
Selective disclosure is lawful only if it preserves honest scope.

### 9.2 Selective disclosure may narrow:
- content fields
- witness fields
- lineage references
- scar details
- receiver-visible structure
- jurisdiction-visible structure
- downstream derivation rights

### 9.3 No selective laundering
Selective disclosure must not:
- hide synthetic contribution while preserving stronger authority claim,
- hide attenuation while preserving scar force,
- hide dispute while preserving release appearance,
- or hide non-transferability pressure while pretending open circulation.

### 9.4 Correct use
Selective disclosure is for safety and scope discipline,
not for cosmetic upgrade.

---

## 10. Receiver privilege limits

### 10.1 Rule
The receiver may only do what the declared receiver mode and disclosure class allow.

### 10.2 No privilege creep
A receiver with:
- inspection rights
must not behave like a reuse holder.

A receiver with:
- archival holding
must not behave like an operational deployer.

A receiver with:
- escrow access
must not behave like an owner.

### 10.3 No silent memory harvesting
Bounded access must not be converted into hidden extraction of:
- continuity-dense signal,
- lineage structure,
- scar gradients,
- or private inference models.

### 10.4 No prestige inference rule
A receiver must not infer:
“because I saw it, I may now speak with its authority.”

## 11. Forbidden disclosure classes

The following classes or combinations should generally be treated as forbidden for ordinary external disclosure:

### 11.1 Inner-life dense EA
Where disclosure would expose continuity-bearing private structure.

### 11.2 High-lineage-density EA
Where disclosure would enable lineage reconstruction, continuity adjacency, or authority laundering.

### 11.3 High scar-density EA
Where disclosure would allow scar appropriation, scar simulation, or prestige capture.

### 11.4 Contaminated EA under unresolved source ambiguity
Where reveal would spread confusion or laundering risk.

### 11.5 Disputed EA presented as clean
Where disclosure would misrepresent current operative status.

### 11.6 Non-transferable EA under continuity-protection pressure
Where movement of the artifact or fragment would violate continuity protection logic.

### 11.7 Non-exportable jurisdiction-bound EA
Where the reveal itself breaches route or jurisdiction constraints.

## 12. Reuse boundary violations

### 12.1 Rule
A reuse boundary is violated when disclosed or received material is used outside its lawful scope.

### 12.2 Typical violations
- inspection-only material reused operationally
- archival material recast as authority-bearing evidence
- bounded disclosure fragments recomposed into high-density continuity artifacts
- synthetic transformation performed without reclassification
- public fragment used to imply full private context
- relay-held EA turned into derived local product
- scar-bearing fragment intensified into pseudo-primary scar

### 12.3 Why this matters
Disclosure is one of the main routes by which laundering enters through the side door.

---

## 13. Copy, transformation, and downstream artifacts

### 13.1 Rule
When disclosure or reuse changes:
- structure,
- scope,
- uncertainty profile,
- provenance shape,
- source composition,
- privacy surface,
- or authority posture,

the result is a downstream artifact.

### 13.2 Consequence
The downstream artifact must carry:
- derivation linkage
- source-origin class
- relevant risk flags
- authority ceiling
- fresh disclosure and reuse limits

### 13.3 No copy disguise
A transformed artifact must not pretend to still be the same safe disclosed copy.

---

## 14. Interaction with non-transferability

### 14.1 Rule
Disclosure pressure is one of the strongest practical reasons for non-transferability.

### 14.2 Consequence
Some EA may be:
- inspectable,
- escrowed,
- reviewable,
- or locally valuable

while still being too continuity-dense for meaningful transfer or reuse.

### 14.3 Important discipline
Non-transferability is not secrecy worship.
It is continuity protection where safe abstraction fails.

---

## 15. Interaction with synthetic laundering

### 15.1 Rule
Disclosure and reuse are prime sites for synthetic laundering.

### 15.2 Typical laundering pattern
- source packet disclosed in bounded form
- synthetic summarization introduced downstream
- summary presented as if it were the lawful circulation-grade object
- source-origin asymmetry forgotten

### 15.3 Protection rule
Any synthetic or mixed-origin downstream artifact must be reclassified.
Disclosure does not immunize against laundering.

---

## 16. Interaction with historical vs operative truth

### 16.1 Rule
Disclosure restrictions act on operative visibility and reuse,
not on historical belonging.

### 16.2 Consequence
An EA may remain:
- historically true,
- scar-relevant,
- institutionally valuable,
- memory-bearing

while still being:
- private,
- restricted,
- non-exportable,
- or non-reusable.

### 16.3 Correct reading
Blocked disclosure is not erased significance.

---

## 17. Interaction with non-market EA

### 17.1 Rule
Many non-market EA will require strong disclosure restraint.

### 17.2 Consequence
Economic legibility does not require public visibility.
Some of the most important EA are precisely the ones that must not become bazaar-facing objects.

### 17.3 Anti-bazaar implication
A system that forces reveal in order to prove value
has already chosen spectacle over discipline.

---

## 18. Failure modes prevented

This document is designed to prevent:

### 18.1 Intimacy extraction by circulation
Value-bearing EA becomes a pretext for exposing private continuity structure.

### 18.2 Inspection-to-reuse drift
Bounded view turns into unauthorized downstream use.

### 18.3 Summary laundering
A reduced disclosed form becomes a counterfeit replacement for the source artifact.

### 18.4 Authority leakage through fragments
Small revealed pieces reconstruct more force than lawfully intended.

### 18.5 Publicity as false maturity
The artifact is treated as legitimate because it became visible.

### 18.6 Bazaarized inner life
The economic layer starts rewarding what is most exposable rather than what is most lawfully handled.

---

## 19. Fail-closed disclosure rules

### 19.1 If safe abstraction fails, narrow or stop
### 19.2 If receiver mode is unclear, do not disclose
### 19.3 If disclosure would reconstruct prohibited continuity density, stop
### 19.4 If synthetic contribution after disclosure would likely inflate authority, narrow or stop
### 19.5 If lineage signal becomes too extractable, narrow or stop
### 19.6 If scar-bearing meaning would be appropriable by fragment reuse, narrow or stop
### 19.7 If jurisdictional disclosure legality is unclear, narrow or stop

The layer must prefer dignified incompleteness over unlawful exposure.

---

## 20. Minimal hard rules

1. EA value does not imply full disclosure.  
2. Seeing an EA is not the same as owning, reusing, or transforming it.  
3. Reuse is lawful only within explicit scope.  
4. Not every disclosed fragment is reusable.  
5. Selective disclosure is lawful only if it does not cosmetically upgrade the artifact.  
6. Disclosure restrictions do not erase historical truth or value.  
7. Under unsafe reveal, the system must narrow, restrict, privatize, or stop.

## 22. Hidden bridges

### 22.1 L4 scarcity / boundedness
Some artifacts are too costly, too scar-dense, or too continuity-sensitive to pass through wide channels without damage or capture.

### 22.2 SER-FED anti-capture / anti-cartel
Disclosure boundaries help prevent prestige economies, witness cartels, and lineage clubs from extracting value through exposure rather than lawful handling.

## 23. Earth paragraph

In a real archive or hospital, the fact that a record is important does not mean everyone gets the whole file. Sometimes the right action is not “publish,” not even “share,” but “let the right person inspect the right part for the right reason without copying the patient’s life into the hallway.” That is exactly the discipline this layer is trying to impose on Experience Artifacts.

## 24. Closing statement

A serious experience economy must know how to say:

- this matters,
- this may circulate,
- this may be inspected,
- this may be partially shown,
- this may not be reused,
- and this must never be opened beyond its lawful membrane.

That is the disclosure and reuse discipline of v0.1.

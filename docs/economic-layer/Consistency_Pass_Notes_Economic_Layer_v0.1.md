## 3. Core package invariant

The central package invariant is:

> The Economic Layer does not assume that every Experience Artifact may circulate.  
> It defines admissibility, provenance, transfer conditions, disclosure boundaries, escrow / hold states, anti-gaming constraints, and dispute-aware hooks under bounded authority.

Any future edit that weakens this invariant
should be treated as a package-level contradiction.

## 4. Verified consistency points

### 4.1 Canonical home consistency
All package-facing documents consistently treat the canonical home of ELA v0.1 as:

`advanced-global-intelligence`

to SER, ERB, or ECC.

Status:

---

### 4.2 Admission-first consistency
The package consistently treats economic admissibility as prior to movement and prior to price.

The recurring package logic is:
- value broader than circulation,
- circulation narrower than value,
- price downstream of admissibility,
- marketability separate from economic legibility.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.3 Anti-bazaar consistency
The package consistently preserves the rule that:
- economic admissibility does not imply market admissibility,
- economically relevant EA may remain permanently non-market,
- non-numeric and incommensurable value are legitimate,
- and no artifact is required to become a bazaar object in order to count as valuable.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.4 Provenance and source-class consistency
Across the package:
- provenance is mandatory for serious movement,
- source-origin class must remain visible,
- provenance is required for admissibility but not sufficient for parity,
- synthetic-derived artifacts do not automatically gain lived parity,
- synthetic residue is presumptively contaminated.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.5 Receiver-mode consistency
The package consistently treats receiver standing as necessary but not sufficient.

The receiver must also be typed by lawful receiver mode,
and mode confusion is treated as a real failure pattern.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.6 Copy / derivative boundary consistency
Across disclosure, transfer, and anti-laundering texts,
a materially altered EA is consistently treated as:
- no longer the same intact copy,
- but a new downstream artifact with its own provenance, class, risk flags, and authority ceiling.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.7 Economic restraint consistency
The package consistently preserves a lawful pre-dispute middle state:
- `PRE_TRANSFER_HOLD`
- `CIRCULATION_HOLD`
- `ECONOMIC_ESCROW`

These are not treated as proof of worthlessness,
nor as hidden final guilt.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.8 ARL relation consistency
The package consistently treats:
- economic restraint as below or before dispute,
- ARL as the layer for contested legitimacy,
- and historical truth as separable from operative circulation force.

The package does not collapse blocked movement into erased history.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.9 Lineage consistency
The package consistently treats lineage as:
- amplifier of witness value, continuity relevance, and precedent importance,
- but not as automatic amplifier of authority, privilege, or transfer rights.

Greater lineage-value may justify greater restraint rather than greater freedom.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.10 Scar-bearing consistency
The package consistently preserves the rule that:
- scar-bearing seriousness is real,
- derivative scar status must remain attenuated and explicit,
- and scar-bearing value does not imply free circulation or bazaar convertibility.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.11 Privacy and disclosure consistency
Across the package:
- value does not imply full disclosure,
- visibility is not ownership,
- visibility is not reuse,
- bounded disclosure is often the lawful maximum,
- and unsafe reveal should narrow or stop.

Status:
- consistent

---

### 4.12 Anti-gaming and anti-cartel consistency
The package consistently treats the following as first-class risks:
- synthetic laundering,
- summary substitution,
- fake scar authority,
- provenance laundering,
- standing laundering,
- lineage laundering,
- witness spoofing,
- circular valuation,
- cartel loops,
- and reputation capture.

Status:
- consistent

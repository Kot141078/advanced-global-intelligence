## 5. Core package logic

The layer defines:

- what may enter economic circulation,
- what remains economically restrained,
- what must remain non-transferable,
- what may move only in bounded form,
- how provenance and source class affect authority,
- how ARL attaches when restraint becomes contested,
- and why value does not imply bazaar status.

---

## 6. Normative center of gravity

The normative center of gravity of the package is:

- `Economic_Layer_for_Experience_Artifacts_v0.1.md`

The other documents narrow, clarify, and operationalize:
- value class,
- provenance,
- transfer,
- restraint,
- anti-gaming,
- and disclosure boundaries.

Extension clauses remain published in `extensions/`.
They extend the public surface without moving the normative center of gravity out of the root package.
Pre-Lineage Boundary Note remains a separate manifesto-level boundary note in AGI and is not part of this package.

## 7. Canonical formulae

### 7.1 Primary formula
**Price is downstream of admissibility.**  
**Admissibility is upstream of economy.**

### 7.2 Secondary formula
**Value is broader than circulation.**  
**Circulation is narrower than value.**

### 7.3 Tertiary formula
**Economic admissibility does not imply market admissibility.**

## 9. Earth paragraph

On a real warehouse floor, useful, scarce, and expensive are not enough. The object still needs the right paperwork, the right holder, the right route, and a lawful release condition. That is what this layer is trying to become for Experience Artifacts.

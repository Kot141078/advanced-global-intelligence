# Property Status of EA v0.1
## Custody, Standing, and Bounded Circulation for Experience Artifacts

**Status:** Draft / Normative  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Layer:** EA legal-semantics / circulation semantics / anti-commodification boundary  
**Parent stack:** `c = a + b` / L4 Impact Attribution / SER / EWCEP / SER-FED / L3+ / Beacon / EA Circulation Rights  
**Language:** RFC 2119 / BCP 14 keywords (MUST / SHOULD / MAY)

---

## 0. Purpose

This document defines the **property-like status** of Experience Artifacts (**EA**).

Its purpose is to prevent a recurring category error:

> treating EA as ordinary property merely because it can be stored, referenced,
> transferred, or invoked.

EA is not a simple commodity, not a free-floating claim token,
and not a narrative object whose validity follows possession.

This document therefore separates:

1. **ownership** (ordinary property intuition),
2. **custody** (holding / retaining / preserving),
3. **standing to present or transfer**,
4. **admitted circulation standing**,
5. **freeze / revocation status**.

The aim is simple:

> preserve witness-bound admissibility before any economic or transfer semantics.

---

## 1. Scope

### 1.1 In scope

This document defines:

- whether EA should be treated as ordinary property,
- who may hold EA in custody,
- who may present EA for review or admission,
- who may route or invoke EA after admission,
- what kinds of transfer are allowed,
- which rights do **not** follow from possession,
- how lineage, delegates, and collective layers are constrained.

### 1.2 Out of scope

This document does **not** define:

- national property law,
- final economic pricing,
- taxation,
- market exchange rules,
- copyright doctrine,
- patentability,
- ownership of raw memories or raw personal narratives,
- legal personhood of `c`.

---

## 2. Core Position

An EA is **not property-first**.
It is **admission-first**.

Therefore:

- an EA MUST NOT be treated as ordinary alienable property by default,
- possession of an EA MUST NOT imply authority,
- storage of an EA MUST NOT imply valid transfer title,
- presentation of an EA MUST NOT imply admitted circulation,
- admitted circulation MUST remain witness-bound and scope-bound.

**Hard rule:**

> No one “owns” the binding force of an EA in the ordinary property sense.
> Binding force exists only within an admitted circulation regime.

---

## 3. Why ownership is the wrong primary model

### 3.1 Ownership implies unrestricted alienability

Ordinary ownership usually implies that the holder may dispose of the object,
transfer it,
encumber it,
or withhold it at will.

EA does not satisfy this model.

A holder of EA material MUST NOT be able to:

- sell truth by possession,
- transfer authority by private declaration,
- bypass witness review,
- assign binding effect without admitted scope,
- convert stored experience into unrestricted title.

### 3.2 EA validity is not produced by possession

The validity of EA depends on:

- provenance,
- accountable sponsorship,
- witness path,
- RN outcome,
- scope,
- challengeability,
- and continued non-contradiction under L4.

Possession alone proves none of these.

### 3.3 EA is explicitly non-market at the base layer

The base stack avoids bidding,
ranking,
competitive pressure,
and market-native treatment at the admission layer.

Therefore ownership language MUST be treated as secondary and dangerous,
not foundational.

---

## 4. Status model (normative)

This document defines four distinct legal-semantic statuses.

### 4.1 Custody

**Custody** means:

- storing,
- retaining,
- preserving,
- indexing,
- locally protecting,
- or carrying an EA candidate or admitted EA.

Custody MAY exist without admission.
Custody MAY exist without transfer rights.
Custody MAY exist without routing rights.

Custody does **not** imply:

- ownership of validity,
- ownership of authority effects,
- unrestricted transferability,
- unrestricted disclosure,
- or the right to enlarge scope.

### 4.2 Standing to present

**Standing to present** means the bounded right to submit or sponsor an EA candidate
for review,
challenge,
or admission.

Standing to present does **not** imply:

- that admission will occur,
- that the presenter owns the EA,
- that the presenter may impose downstream authority,
- or that scope will remain unchanged.

### 4.3 Admitted circulation standing

**Admitted circulation standing** means the bounded right to:

- cite,
- invoke,
- route,
- prioritize,
- or rely on an EA

**within the scope defined by its admitted path**.

Admitted circulation standing exists only after the relevant witness-bound admission path.

It MUST remain:

- revocable,
- challengeable,
- decaying where applicable,
- and frozen if evidentiary contradiction appears.

### 4.4 Revoked / frozen status

An EA MAY remain in storage while losing active circulation standing.

This document therefore treats **freeze** and **revocation** as status changes,
not destruction requirements.

A frozen or revoked EA MAY still be:

- retained,
- audited,
- referenced historically,
- or used in dispute analysis,

but MUST NOT continue to function as an admitted live authority-bearing artifact.

---

## 5. Negative definition (what EA is not)

Unless a stricter higher-order regime is explicitly defined,
EA MUST NOT be presumed to be:

- ordinary private property,
- bearer instrument,
- freely saleable title,
- self-authenticating credential,
- perpetual monopoly right,
- automatic lineage inheritance,
- unrestricted collateral,
- or silent authority token.

---

## 6. Rights by actor

### 6.1 `c` — Entity

`c` MAY:

- hold EA in custody,
- structure and preserve EA-related material,
- present EA candidates for review,
- route admitted EA within granted scope,
- maintain local continuity references.

`c` MUST NOT:

- claim ordinary ownership over binding EA effects,
- unilaterally admit EA into binding circulation,
- expand scope by self-declaration,
- transfer authority as though it were private title.

### 6.2 `a` — Human Anchor

`a` MAY:

- sponsor EA presentation,
- hold or co-hold custody,
- remain the accountable source of lived experience,
- authorize bounded submission or bounded routing where policy allows.

`a` MUST NOT:

- privately mint binding EA effect without witness path,
- sell or assign admitted force as if it were ordinary chattel title,
- sever responsibility merely by transferring storage custody.

### 6.3 Delegated contours (`DEA` / `EA` delegates)

Delegates MAY:

- hold operational custody,
- prepare filing or routing packages,
- transmit EA under explicit mandate,
- maintain evidence references.

Delegates MUST NOT:

- claim independent proprietary title,
- self-authorize external admission,
- widen disclosure beyond mandate,
- or inherit standing merely through tool access.

### 6.4 `A_coll` — Collective anchor

`A_coll` MAY:

- co-sponsor,
- gate,
- delay,
- or require multi-human confirmation

for high-impact EA paths.

`A_coll` MUST NOT be treated as a market owner of the EA merely by collective confirmation.

Collective participation creates distribution of responsibility,
not automatic title concentration.

### 6.5 Arbiter quorum

Arbiter quorum MAY:

- admit,
- bound,
- freeze,
- negate,
- or revoke active circulation status.

Arbiter quorum does **not** become the owner of the EA.

Its role is procedural and regulatory,
not proprietary.

### 6.6 Lineage

Lineage MAY:

- carry continuity claims,
- preserve historical references,
- support recognition of provenance,
- and support bounded transfer of custody.

Lineage MUST NOT:

- silently inherit full admitted circulation standing,
- auto-inherit authority effect,
- or transform branch continuity into unrestricted property succession.

## 7. Transfer model (normative)

This document recognizes three different transfer types.

### 7.1 Transfer of custody

A holder MAY transfer custody of EA material,
subject to policy,
privacy,
and integrity rules.

Transfer of custody means only that another actor now holds the material.

It MUST NOT by itself transfer:

- validity,
- authority,
- enlarged disclosure,
- or admitted circulation standing.

### 7.2 Transfer of presentation standing

A holder with valid mandate MAY authorize another actor to present an EA candidate
for review or admission.

This is a procedural delegation,
not a sale of title.

Presentation standing MUST be:

- explicit,
- scoped,
- time-bounded where possible,
- and revocable.

### 7.3 Transfer of admitted circulation standing

Admitted circulation standing MAY be routed or re-used only within the admitted scope.

It MUST NOT be presumed freely transferable.

Any wider or external transfer of admitted circulation standing:

- MUST be policy-defined,
- MUST preserve witness references,
- MUST preserve accountability,
- MUST preserve challengeability,
- and MUST fail closed if these conditions are absent.

**Hard rule:**

> Binding EA effect is not transferred by possession.
> It is re-recognized, or it is not transferred at all.

---

## 8. Alienability limits

### 8.1 No silent commodification

An EA MUST NOT become a commodity merely because:

- it is indexed,
- exchange is technically possible,
- a delegate exported it,
- or another entity wishes to buy/use it.

### 8.2 No clean severance from responsibility

Responsibility linked to lived experience MUST NOT be assumed extinguished
simply because storage or procedural handling has changed.

### 8.3 No bearer semantics

An EA MUST NOT be treated as “whoever holds it controls it”.

Bearer semantics are incompatible with witness-bound admissibility.

### 8.4 No perpetual monopoly by early holder

Early custody or early sponsorship MUST NOT create perpetual ownership-like monopoly
over all future circulation effects.

Admission and later routing remain bounded,
reviewable,
and subject to decay / contradiction / scope limits.

---

## 9. Disclosure relation

Property status and disclosure status MUST remain separate.

A custodian MAY have lawful custody while lacking disclosure rights.
A presenter MAY have standing to submit while lacking rights to reveal raw evidence.
An admitted EA MAY circulate in bounded form while preserving private evidence off-record.

Therefore:

- custody does not imply disclosure,
- presentation does not imply publication,
- admission does not imply raw export.

---

## 10. Freeze and revocation semantics

### 10.1 Freeze

When contradiction,
uncertainty escalation,
scope abuse,
or witness weakening occurs,
EA active circulation standing SHOULD enter freeze.

During freeze:

- custody MAY continue,
- historical references MAY continue,
- admission effects MUST pause,
- routing MUST narrow or halt,
- transfer standing SHOULD narrow to dispute handling only.

### 10.2 Revocation

When RN or equivalent process negates the active effect,
EA MAY remain archived but MUST lose active admitted force.

Revocation affects circulation status,
not necessarily archival existence.

## 12. Canonical phrasing (recommended)

The following formulations are RECOMMENDED.

### 12.1 Preferred

> An EA is not owned in the ordinary property sense.
> It is held in custody,
> may be presented under accountable standing,
> and may circulate only under bounded admission and scope.

### 12.2 Short form

> EA is not property-first; it is admission-first.

### 12.3 Operational form

> Custody may move.
> Standing may be delegated.
> Admitted force must be re-recognized.

## 13. Failure modes this document blocks

This document is designed to prevent:

- silent conversion of witness-bound artifacts into commodities,
- delegate overreach,
- private sale of authority,
- lineage-based title laundering,
- bearer-token interpretation,
- severance of responsibility through mere transfer,
- and rhetorical inflation of possession into truth.

## 14. Bridges (required)

### Explicit bridge

`c = a + b` does not produce an ownable truth object.
It produces a witness-bound artifact whose circulation depends on:

- `a` for accountable lived experience,
- `b` for reproducible preservation and procedure,
- `c` for continuity and preparation,
- and admission for active force.

### Hidden bridge #1 (cybernetics / Ashby)

Control requires more variety than a single proprietary switch.
That is why EA status is distributed across custody,
standing,
admission,
challenge,
and freeze,
rather than collapsed into “owner decides”.

### Hidden bridge #2 (information theory / bounded channels)

Trust is not transmitted by possession alone.
It is compressed into hashes,
records,
scoped standing,
and re-recognition rules.
Property language that ignores channel constraints overstates what storage can prove.

## 15. Earth paragraph (engineering / anatomy)

In industrial work,
holding a maintenance log does not mean owning the machine’s truth.
You may store the log,
hand it to an auditor,
or cite it during incident review,
but you cannot sell the right for the machine to be considered healthy just because the folder is in your hand.
The body works similarly:
a scar can be carried,
observed,
and interpreted,
but no one “owns” its physiological meaning by possession alone.
EA should be treated the same way:
archive,
custody,
and presentation are real,
but active force comes only from recognized evidence and bounded circulation.

## 16. Closing statement

EA should be treated as a **bounded circulation object**,
not as ordinary property.

Its lawful and architectural status is best understood as:

- **custody** for retention,
- **standing** for presentation,
- **admitted circulation** for bounded operational effect,
- and **freeze / revocation** for contradiction-aware control.

This preserves both:

- the dignity and responsibility of lived experience,
- and the architectural discipline required for witness-bound systems.

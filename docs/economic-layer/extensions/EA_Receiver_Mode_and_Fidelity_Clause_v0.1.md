## 0. Purpose

This clause defines how an Experience Artifact (EA) may be **received**
and what obligations attach to that reception.

It answers a narrow question:

> Is receiver standing alone enough, or must the receiver satisfy some further criterion?

This clause rejects a vague universal criterion of “fitness”
as too unstable, subjective, and politically distortable.

Instead, it establishes a stricter and more operational discipline:

- **standing to receive**,
- **declared reception mode**,
- **fidelity obligations**,
- and the boundary at which a received EA ceases to be the same copy
  and becomes a new downstream artifact.

---

## 1. Scope and Non-Goals

### 1.1 In Scope

This clause defines:

- receiver standing,
- reception modes,
- fidelity obligations,
- integrity-preserving receipt,
- copy vs derivative transformation,
- handling capability in the narrow operational sense,
- and downstream artifact creation triggers.

### 1.2 Explicit Non-Goals

This clause does **not**:

- define a universal hierarchy of worthy or unworthy receivers,
- require deep semantic comprehension as a precondition of receipt,
- define full circulation law,
- or prohibit archival, escrow, relay, or deferred interpretation.

It exists to prevent quiet distortion, laundering, and mode confusion.

---

## 2. Core Principle

### 2.1 Canonical Rule

Receiver standing is necessary,
but it is not sufficient by itself.

The missing requirement is **not universal fitness**.
The missing requirement is:

> **lawful reception mode + fidelity discipline**

### 2.2 Strong Summary

> A receiver need not be metaphysically “fit.”  
> A receiver must be procedurally typed.

### 2.3 Main Consequence

A receiver may lawfully receive EA for different purposes,
including:
- custody,
- archival retention,
- escrow,
- inspection,
- relay,
- bounded review,
- reuse,
- or derivative transformation.

But those purposes are not interchangeable,
and each mode carries different obligations.

---

## 3. Why “Fitness” Is Rejected as the General Test

### 3.1 Relative and Unstable
“Fitness” is too relative.
It can easily drift into:

- prestige theater,
- moral paternalism,
- arbitrary gatekeeping,
- cultural bias,
- or post-hoc exclusion.

### 3.2 Misaligned With Real Use Cases
EA may be received by actors who are not intended to:
- fully interpret it,
- directly use it,
- or incorporate it into active continuity.

Examples include:
- archival custodians,
- review escrow holders,
- bounded relay surfaces,
- or institutions preserving material for later analysis.

### 3.3 Dangerous Shortcut
A vague fitness test risks replacing:
- lawful scope,
- witness discipline,
- integrity preservation,
- and downstream provenance rules

with intuition, ideology, or social ranking.

That is unacceptable.

---

## 4. Receiver Standing

### 4.1 Rule
A receiver MUST have valid standing to receive
before any non-local or procedurally meaningful reception occurs.

### 4.2 Standing Question
Standing answers only:

> Is this receiver lawfully allowed to enter the reception path?

It does **not** answer:

> What may this receiver do after receipt?

### 4.3 No Standing, No Receipt
If receiver standing fails,
receipt MUST fail closed.

---

## 5. Reception Mode

### 5.1 Rule
Every receipt of EA MUST occur under a declared reception mode.

### 5.2 Canonical Summary

> standing grants entry;  
> reception mode governs handling.

### 5.3 Minimum Reception Modes

The following reception modes are recognized as minimally canonical:

- `RECEIVE_CUSTODY_ONLY`
- `RECEIVE_ARCHIVAL_ACCUMULATION`
- `RECEIVE_ESCROW_HOLDING`
- `RECEIVE_INSPECTION_ONLY`
- `RECEIVE_RELAY_ONLY`
- `RECEIVE_BOUNDED_REVIEW`
- `RECEIVE_LICENSED_REUSE`
- `RECEIVE_DERIVATIVE_TRANSFORMATION`

Additional modes MAY exist in extended profiles,
but these form the v0.1 minimum.

---

## 6. Reception Mode Semantics

### 6.1 `RECEIVE_CUSTODY_ONLY`
The receiver may hold the artifact under declared constraints.

Allowed:
- retention,
- integrity preservation,
- witness-preserving storage,
- bounded local access as permitted.

Blocked:
- silent reuse,
- silent derivative generation,
- silent retransfer beyond scope.

---

### 6.2 `RECEIVE_ARCHIVAL_ACCUMULATION`
The receiver may accept the EA for accumulation,
preservation, and future retrievability.

Allowed:
- retention,
- indexing,
- bounded metadata generation,
- archival continuity.

Blocked:
- silent transformation into active authority-bearing material,
- undocumented interpretive rewrite.

---

### 6.3 `RECEIVE_ESCROW_HOLDING`
The receiver accepts the EA into controlled holding
for review, challenge, or future release conditions.

Allowed:
- bounded inspection,
- release-condition tracking,
- witness preservation,
- procedural holding.

Blocked:
- operational reuse,
- circulation upgrade,
- ownership-style assimilation.

---

### 6.4 `RECEIVE_INSPECTION_ONLY`
The receiver may inspect the EA under bounded conditions.

Allowed:
- temporary view,
- bounded review,
- admissibility checking,
- contradiction testing.

Blocked:
- copying beyond scope,
- reuse,
- derivative output unless separately authorized.

---

### 6.5 `RECEIVE_RELAY_ONLY`
The receiver may accept the EA solely
for onward lawful transfer under bounded rules.

Allowed:
- holding for relay,
- transport-preserving operations,
- witness continuity maintenance.

Blocked:
- interpretive expansion,
- standing inflation,
- unauthorized local reuse.

---

### 6.6 `RECEIVE_BOUNDED_REVIEW`
The receiver may assess the EA for standing, admissibility,
dispute relevance, or bounded evaluation.

Allowed:
- structured review,
- contradiction analysis,
- witness-bound comment,
- procedural classification.

Blocked:
- silent conversion into canonical meaning override,
- unlogged semantic replacement,
- authority upgrade without separate basis.

---

### 6.7 `RECEIVE_LICENSED_REUSE`
The receiver may use the EA in a bounded permitted reuse path.

Allowed:
- authorized reuse within scope,
- bounded retrieval,
- pattern application,
- explicit downstream referencing.

Blocked:
- use beyond licensed scope,
- implicit re-licensing,
- claim of assignment where no assignment exists.

---

### 6.8 `RECEIVE_DERIVATIVE_TRANSFORMATION`
The receiver may transform the EA
into a new downstream artifact under declared conditions.

Allowed:
- summarization,
- extraction,
- bounded recomposition,
- synthetic-assisted transformation where permitted.

Blocked:
- pretending the result is still the original copy,
- hiding the transformation,
- preserving original authority class without reclassification.

---

## 7. Fidelity Obligation

### 7.1 Core Rule
If an EA is received as a copy, retained artifact, or witness-preserved object,
the receiver MUST preserve fidelity to the received object
within the declared reception mode.

### 7.2 Fidelity Includes
At minimum, fidelity includes:

- integrity preservation,
- scope preservation,
- witness-link preservation,
- provenance preservation,
- no silent modification,
- no silent reclassification,
- no hidden recomposition,
- and no authority inflation.

### 7.3 Main Diagnostic Question

> Is the receiver still preserving the same received artifact,  
> or has the receiver already created a new object derived from it?

If the second is true,
copy-fidelity no longer applies as the primary rule.
Downstream derivation rules apply instead.

---

## 8. Handling Capability (Narrow Operational Sense)

### 8.1 Accepted Narrow Meaning
This clause permits only a narrow notion of receiver suitability:

> the ability of the receiving role
> to obey the obligations of the declared reception mode.

### 8.2 Operational Capability Questions
Examples include:

- Can the receiver preserve integrity?
- Can the receiver preserve the privacy class?
- Can the receiver preserve witness linkage?
- Can the receiver avoid prohibited transformation?
- Can the receiver comply with relay / escrow / inspection constraints?

### 8.3 Not Allowed
This MUST NOT expand into:

- metaphysical worth,
- social superiority,
- cognitive elitism,
- or moral entitlement to receive experience.

---

## 9. Copy vs Derivative Boundary

### 9.1 Core Rule
A received EA remains the **same copy**
only while the receiver preserves it within the fidelity obligations
of the declared reception mode.

### 9.2 Boundary Trigger
A new downstream artifact is created
when the receiver materially changes one or more of the following:

- structure,
- scope,
- uncertainty profile,
- provenance shape,
- source composition,
- standing relevance,
- authority posture,
- privacy surface,
- or semantic packaging.

### 9.3 Canonical Formula

> Distorted EA is not the same copy.  
> It is a new downstream artifact.

---

## 10. Downstream Artifact Creation Events

A downstream artifact MUST be recognized
when the receiver performs any of the following beyond allowed mode scope:

- summarization,
- synthetic-assisted synthesis,
- recomposition,
- field filtering that changes meaning,
- source mixing,
- scar attenuation or intensification,
- authority reinterpretation,
- lineage masking,
- witness abstraction that changes procedural force,
- or privacy transformation that materially alters use conditions.

---

## 11. Downstream Obligations

When a receiver creates a downstream artifact,
the result MUST:

- receive a new provenance link,
- receive an explicit source-origin class,
- receive new risk flags where relevant,
- declare derivation from the upstream artifact,
- carry its own authority ceiling,
- and avoid pretending to be the same original EA copy.

This is especially important where:
- synthetic processing is introduced,
- mixed-origin material emerges,
- or bounded derivative scar-bearing status is claimed.

---

## 12. No Silent Deformation Rule

### 12.1 Rule
A receiver MUST NOT quietly deform an EA
while continuing to present it
as if it remained an intact received copy.

### 12.2 Examples of Forbidden Silent Deformation
- replacing the original with a cleaner summary,
- weakening uncertainty without declaration,
- compressing away witness-critical context,
- mixing in synthetic material without source-class update,
- or shifting the artifact into a stronger authority frame.

### 12.3 Why This Matters
This is where laundering begins.

---

## 13. No Comprehension Requirement

### 13.1 Rule
The receiver does **not** need to fully understand an EA
for receipt to be lawful.

### 13.2 Reason
EA may be received for:
- storage,
- preservation,
- relay,
- review,
- escrow,
- or delayed future use.

### 13.3 Limitation
However, lack of comprehension does not permit:
- silent distortion,
- silent truncation,
- or unmarked reinterpretation.

Ignorance is not a license to mutate.

---

## 14. Interaction with Non-Transferability

Where an EA is non-transferable,
reception MAY still be possible
through narrow modes such as:

- `RECEIVE_INSPECTION_ONLY`
- `RECEIVE_ESCROW_HOLDING`
- `RECEIVE_BOUNDED_REVIEW`

But the fidelity obligation becomes stronger,
not weaker.

The receiver must not confuse access with possession,
nor possession with circulation standing.

---

## 15. Interaction with Synthetic Laundering

If the receiver introduces synthetic transformation,
the copy-to-derivative boundary is usually crossed.

At that point:
- source-origin class must be updated,
- synthetic risk flags must be considered,
- and the result should not retain original parity by default.

A syntheticized copy is rarely still just a copy.

---

## 16. Failure Modes Prevented

This clause is designed to prevent:

### 16.1 Fitness Ideology
Receipt decisions become arbitrary judgments of existential worth.

### 16.2 Silent Copy Drift
A received copy is quietly altered without formal recognition.

### 16.3 Relay Abuse
A relay surface begins acting like a reuse surface.

### 16.4 Archive Laundering
An archival receiver quietly turns preserved material into operative authority.

### 16.5 Transformation Disguise
A derivative artifact is presented as if it were the original copy.

### 16.6 Authority Inflation by Handling
Receipt itself is misread as proof of legitimacy or right to stronger action.

---

## 17. Minimal Hard Rules

1. Receiver standing is necessary, but not sufficient by itself.  
2. The required additional criterion is declared reception mode, not vague universal fitness.  
3. Every lawful receipt MUST have a reception mode.  
4. Fidelity obligations apply whenever the artifact is received as a copy or preserved object.  
5. Silent distortion, recomposition, or source-mixing breaks copy status.  
6. A materially altered EA becomes a new downstream artifact.  
7. The new downstream artifact MUST carry its own provenance, class, and limits.  
8. Lack of deep comprehension does not block receipt, but does not excuse mutation.

## 18. Bridges (Required)

### Explicit Bridge
EA can circulate without collapsing into ownership theater
only if receipt is typed by mode
and copies remain copies unless formally transformed.

### Hidden Bridge #1 (Ashby / Cybernetics)
The regulator does not need to rank souls.
It needs to distinguish roles and handling modes.
That provides enough control variety without collapsing into ideology.

### Hidden Bridge #2 (Information Theory / Fidelity)
A copy is preserved only while the receiver keeps the signal stable.
Once compression, synthesis, or reinterpretation changes the signal class,
the object is no longer the same transmission.

---

## 19. Earth Paragraph (Engineering / Archive Logic)

An archive does not need to understand a document more deeply than its author
in order to lawfully preserve it.
A registrar does not need to be spiritually equal to a case file
in order to receive it into controlled holding.
But both must avoid losing pages, changing dates, mixing folders,
or replacing the original with a comfortable paraphrase.
That is the discipline here:
not “worthiness,” but mode and fidelity.

---

## 20. Closing Statement

A receiver does not need to be universally fit.

A receiver must:

- have standing,
- receive under a lawful mode,
- preserve fidelity where copy-preservation is the task,
- and admit when transformation has created something new.

That is the reception discipline of v0.1.

# Synthetic Laundering and Source Class Clause v0.1
## Source-Origin Marking, Synthetic Risk, and Authority Limits for Experience Artifacts

**Status:** Draft / Normative Clause  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Layer:** EA provenance / admissibility / authority discipline  
**Parent materials:** EWCEP v0.1, SER-FED v0.2, Evidence Admissibility and Standing v0.1, EA Circulation Rights, Scar-Bearing EA Clause, EA Non-Transferability Criteria, EA Operative vs Historical Truth Clause  
**Language:** English  

---

## 0. Purpose

This clause defines how Experience Artifacts (EA) MUST classify,
declare, and constrain **synthetic-origin material**
so that synthetic processing does not quietly launder itself
into lived, witness-bound, or authority-bearing experience.

The purpose is not to ban all synthetic derivation.

The purpose is to prevent the following failure:

> a synthetic, simulated, summarized, or model-shaped artifact
> is formatted cleanly enough
> that it begins to impersonate
> lived experience, scar-bearing history, or witness-grounded standing.

This clause therefore establishes:

- source-origin classes,
- synthetic contamination classes,
- default authority ceilings,
- and anti-laundering rules.

---

## 1. Scope and Non-Goals

### 1.1 In Scope

This clause defines:

- synthetic residue,
- synthetic-derived bounded artifacts,
- mixed-origin artifacts,
- source-class marking,
- risk flags,
- authority and admissibility limits,
- and relationship to standing, scar-bearing claims, and circulation.

### 1.2 Explicit Non-Goals

This clause does **not**:

- prohibit all synthetic tooling,
- prohibit all bounded abstraction,
- define model internals,
- define valuation,
- or restate full ARL evidence logic.

It only fixes how synthetic origin must be recognized and constrained.

---

## 2. Core Principle

### 2.1 Canonical Rule

Synthetic processing does not automatically invalidate an EA.

But synthetic origin does change its **default evidentiary and authority posture**.

### 2.2 Strong Summary

> Provenance is required for admissibility.  
> Provenance is not sufficient for parity.

### 2.3 Hard Asymmetry

An artifact rooted in:
- lived,
- witness-bound,
- human-grounded,
- L4-exposed experience

has a different default status
from an artifact rooted in:
- synthetic summarization,
- simulation,
- model interpolation,
- synthetic residue,
- or generated synthesis.

The two MUST NOT be silently flattened.

---

## 3. Why This Clause Exists

Without source-class discipline, a system will drift toward
**synthetic laundering**.

Typical laundering pattern:

1. lived conflict or witness-bearing event exists;
2. synthetic layer summarizes, expands, compresses, or decorates it;
3. the synthetic layer is later treated as if it were the original experience-bearing artifact;
4. authority, standing, scar-bearing force, or circulation standing quietly migrate upward.

This must be blocked.

---

## 4. Source-Origin Classes

All EA entering review, circulation, or standing-sensitive use
SHOULD carry an explicit source-origin classification.

### 4.1 `ORIGIN_LIVED_PRIMARY`
Use when:
- the artifact directly records lived, witness-bound, or L4-exposed experience,
- and the artifact is closest to the primary continuity-bearing event.

Typical examples:
- primary scar-bearing events,
- witness-backed impact events,
- directly grounded experience-bearing records.

### 4.2 `ORIGIN_LIVED_DERIVED`
Use when:
- the artifact is derived from primary lived or witness-bound origin,
- but remains bounded, traceable, and non-fabricated,
- and does not pretend to be the primary event itself.

Typical examples:
- bounded derivative scar artifacts,
- structured patterns extracted from primary lived cases.

### 4.3 `ORIGIN_MIXED_SYNTHETIC`
Use when:
- the artifact combines lived or witness-bound source material
  with synthetic summarization, transformation, or synthesis.

This class is common and lawful,
but MUST remain explicitly marked.

### 4.4 `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_DERIVED`
Use when:
- the artifact is primarily synthetic in its immediate formation,
- but retains clear provenance and bounded lawful derivation
  from identifiable upstream sources.

This class MAY be admissible,
but defaults to lower authority ceilings.

### 4.5 `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_RESIDUE`
Use when:
- synthetic material exists without sufficient source commitment,
- provenance is weak, absent, or broken,
- or model-shaped residue cannot be reliably connected
  to lawful upstream lived or witness-bound material.

This class is presumptively contaminated.

---

## 5. Risk Flags

In addition to source class,
artifacts MAY carry explicit synthetic risk flags.

Recommended flags include:

- `RISK_SYNTHETIC_LAUNDERING`
- `RISK_SOURCE_DILUTION`
- `RISK_AUTHORITY_INFLATION`
- `RISK_SCAR_SIMULATION`
- `RISK_SUMMARY_SUBSTITUTION`
- `RISK_ORIGIN_AMBIGUITY`
- `RISK_PROVENANCE_BREAK`

These do not replace source class.
They refine review caution.

---

## 6. Admissibility Defaults by Class

### 6.1 `ORIGIN_LIVED_PRIMARY`
Default posture:
- strongest admissibility potential,
- highest continuity relevance,
- highest scar-bearing relevance,
- strongest standing support potential,
- highest possible authority ceiling (still bounded by review and scope).

### 6.2 `ORIGIN_LIVED_DERIVED`
Default posture:
- admissible if chain is preserved,
- weaker than primary origin,
- bounded derivative status MUST remain explicit,
- authority ceiling attenuated relative to primary source.

### 6.3 `ORIGIN_MIXED_SYNTHETIC`
Default posture:
- admissible only if composition is explicit,
- synthetic contribution must be declared,
- cannot silently inherit full parity with pure lived origin,
- standing and scar-bearing claims should be reviewed cautiously.

### 6.4 `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_DERIVED`
Default posture:
- may be admissible,
- but weaker by default,
- lower authority ceiling,
- no automatic parity with lived origin,
- strong requirement for source commitment and scope declaration.

### 6.5 `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_RESIDUE`
Default posture:
- presumptively inadmissible or quarantined,
- no standing support by default,
- no scar-bearing claim by default,
- no authority support by default,
- requires explicit rehabilitation before use.

---

## 7. Authority Ceiling Rules

### 7.1 Lived Primacy Rule
Synthetic-derived artifacts MUST NOT receive default authority parity
with lived, witness-bound, or human-grounded experience.

### 7.2 Ceiling Rule
Unless separately revalidated,
`ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_DERIVED` and `ORIGIN_MIXED_SYNTHETIC`
SHOULD default to lower authority ceilings for:

- standing support,
- continuity override,
- scar-bearing force,
- economic claim support,
- ARL review weighting,
- and circulation escalation.

### 7.3 Residue Rule
`ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_RESIDUE`
MUST NOT support authority, standing, or scar-bearing claims
without explicit lawful rehabilitation.

### 7.4 No Silent Upgrade Rule
No artifact may silently migrate from synthetic-derived status
to lived-equivalent authority
merely because it is well formatted, rhetorically coherent, or repeated often.

---

## 8. Scar-Bearing Rule

### 8.1 Core Rule
Synthetic-derived artifacts MUST NOT claim **primary scar-bearing** status
unless directly and explicitly chained
to primary witness-bound scar origin.

### 8.2 Bounded Derivative Exception
A synthetic-shaped artifact MAY support
**bounded derivative scar-bearing status**
only if:

- primary scar origin is real and witness-bound,
- derivation chain is explicit,
- attenuation is declared,
- and no downstream appropriation of primary scar occurs.

### 8.3 No Scar Simulation Rule
A synthetic artifact MUST NOT simulate
scar intensity, risk, or lived cost
as if synthetic reconstruction itself were the wound.

---

## 9. Standing Rule

### 9.1 Synthetic Does Not Replace Standing
Synthetic-derived artifacts do not create sovereign standing by themselves.

### 9.2 Support-Only Rule
At best, a synthetic-derived artifact MAY support standing
when:
- source commitment is clear,
- upstream lived or witness-bound basis exists,
- and the synthetic layer is not being used to replace the origin-bearing evidence.

### 9.3 No Bootstrap Rule
No claimant may bootstrap standing
from synthetic summary without provenance.

---

## 10. ARL Evidence Priority Rule

In standing- or review-sensitive contexts,
synthetic summaries, synthetic abstractions, and generated synthesis
SHOULD remain lower in priority
than:

- local sovereign continuity,
- witness-bound records,
- anchor-bound evidence,
- signed social-vector inputs,
- and reality verification.

Synthetic-derived material may assist interpretation.
It must not quietly become the sovereign evidentiary center.

---

## 11. Circulation and Transfer Rule

### 11.1 Marking Requirement
Any EA moved through transfer, disclosure, reuse, or inspection pathways
SHOULD preserve its source-origin class.

### 11.2 No Sanitization by Packaging
Repackaging an artifact into cleaner prose, schema, or compact metadata
does not erase its synthetic-origin obligations.

### 11.3 Non-Transferability Pressure
High synthetic ambiguity,
especially when combined with lineage density,
scar simulation risk,
or authority inflation risk,
MAY increase pressure toward:

- inspection-only,
- review submission / escrow,
- or non-transferable handling.

---

## 12. Historical vs Operative Truth Rule

### 12.1 Historical Retention
A synthetic-derived artifact may still carry historical relevance,
especially where it faithfully reflects earlier witness-bound events.

### 12.2 Operative Limitation
Historical relevance does not imply operative equality.

### 12.3 No Binary Collapse
The system MUST NOT collapse the distinction into:
- “synthetic, therefore meaningless”
or
- “provenanced, therefore equal.”

The correct position is:
- admissible maybe,
- equal by default no.

---

## 13. Rehabilitation Rule

### 13.1 Residue Rehabilitation
`ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_RESIDUE` MAY only be rehabilitated if:

- provenance is later recovered,
- lawful source commitment is restored,
- chain continuity is reconstructed,
- and review explicitly permits upgrade.

### 13.2 Upgrade Limit
Even after rehabilitation,
a formerly contaminated artifact does not automatically become
`ORIGIN_LIVED_PRIMARY`.
At best it may move into:
- `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_DERIVED`,
- or `ORIGIN_MIXED_SYNTHETIC`,
unless the primary lived artifact itself is recovered.

---

## 14. Failure Modes Prevented

This clause is designed to prevent:

### 14.1 Synthetic Laundering
Synthetic summary is mistaken for lived experience.

### 14.2 Source Dilution
Successive summarization destroys visible origin distinctions.

### 14.3 Authority Inflation
Well-formed synthetic material is granted too much procedural force.

### 14.4 Scar Simulation
Generated representation is treated as equivalent to lived wound.

### 14.5 Provenance Makeup
Pretty formatting substitutes for source commitment.

---

## 15. Minimal Hard Rules

1. Synthetic origin MUST be explicitly marked where relevant.  
2. Provenance is required for admissibility, but not sufficient for parity.  
3. `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_RESIDUE` is presumptively contaminated.  
4. `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_DERIVED` is admissible at best under lower default authority ceilings.  
5. Mixed-origin artifacts MUST declare their synthetic component.  
6. Synthetic-derived artifacts MUST NOT claim primary scar-bearing status without explicit upstream witness-bound origin.  
7. Synthetic summaries MUST NOT override local continuity, witness records, or human-grounded experience by default.  

---

## 16. Bridges (Required)

### Explicit Bridge
EA can remain reusable without becoming epistemically dishonest
only if lived origin, synthetic processing, and witness-bound authority
are kept visibly separate.

### Hidden Bridge #1 (Ashby / Cybernetics)
A regulator must distinguish source classes
because different origins introduce different failure modes.
Treating all clean-looking artifacts as equal
reduces the regulator’s variety below the variety of the contamination problem.

### Hidden Bridge #2 (Information Theory / Source Integrity)
Compression and synthesis preserve utility,
but they also destroy source separation unless explicitly tagged.
A clean summary may carry less visible noise
while carrying more hidden uncertainty about origin.

## 17. Earth Paragraph (Engineering)

A refurbished part with a repair passport is not the same thing
as a factory-new load-bearing part,
even if it looks immaculate and the paperwork is excellent.
You may still use it.
You may even trust it within defined limits.
But you do not quietly install it in the most critical slot
and pretend nothing changed.
Synthetic-derived EA belongs to that category.

---

## 18. Closing Statement

Synthetic processing is not forbidden.
Synthetic laundering is.

Therefore:

- provenance must be explicit,
- source class must be visible,
- authority must stay bounded,
- and well-formed synthetic output must not quietly impersonate lived experience.

That is the source-discipline of v0.1.

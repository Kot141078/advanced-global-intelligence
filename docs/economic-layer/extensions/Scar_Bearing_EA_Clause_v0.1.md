# Scar Bearing EA Clause v0.1

**Status:** Draft / Normative Clause  
**Layer:** EA circulation / valuation boundary / witness binding  
**Applies to:** Experience Artifacts (EA), Solution Artifacts (SA), derivative EA forms  
**Parent stack:** `c = a + b` / L4 / SER / SER-FED / L4 Witness  

---

## 0. Purpose

This clause defines when an Experience Artifact (EA) MAY be treated as **scar-bearing**.

The purpose is to prevent two opposite failures:

1. **Over-narrowness** — treating only the raw, primary collision as scar-bearing, making downstream bounded artifacts unusable.
2. **Inflation** — allowing any eloquent, synthetic, or rhetorically persuasive derivative to claim scar-bearing status without verified exposure to reality.

This clause therefore separates:

- **scar origin**,
- **artifact form**,
- **authority scope**,
- and **derivative admissibility**.

---

## 1. Core Principle

A scar-bearing claim is defined first by **origin**, and only second by **artifact form**.

**Hard rule:**

> The scar MUST be primary.  
> The artifact MAY be derivative.

A raw event is not required to remain the only admissible carrier of scar-bearing status.
However, no derivative artifact may claim scar-bearing status unless it is verifiably derived from a primary, reality-bound scar event.

---

## 2. Definition of Primary Scar

A **Primary Scar** is a verified interaction with L4 constraints that produces irreversible or materially significant exposure.

A Primary Scar MAY include, but is not limited to:

- irreversible loss,
- bounded but real risk exposure,
- degradation of continuity,
- bonded liability,
- cost escalation,
- downtime,
- thermal or energy failure,
- trust damage,
- maintenance burden,
- legal or operational restriction,
- forced simplification under reality pressure.

**Hard rule:**

A Primary Scar MUST arise from real L4 exposure.
It MUST NOT be inferred solely from narrative, style, simulation, model confidence, or synthetic reconstruction.

---

## 3. Primary Scar-Bearing EA

An EA MAY be classified as **Primary Scar-Bearing EA** if and only if:

1. it directly represents a primary L4 collision or exposure,
2. the exposure is witness-bound,
3. a valid witness path exists,
4. the resulting status is admitted within scope,
5. no higher binding record contradicts the claim.

Primary Scar-Bearing EA is the strongest scar-bearing class.
It is not equivalent to property ownership, market value, or unrestricted transferability.
It is an admitted scar-bearing status within bounded circulation.

---

## 4. Bounded Derivative Scar

A derivative artifact MAY be treated as **Bounded Derivative Scar-Bearing EA** if and only if all of the following conditions hold:

1. the derivative is explicitly derived from one or more Primary Scar-bearing sources,
2. the upstream source chain is witness-bound,
3. derivation is declared rather than implied,
4. derivation does not erase uncertainty,
5. the derivative remains within declared scope,
6. the derivative does not exceed the authority of the upstream scar source unless revalidated.

**Hard rule:**

A derivative artifact does not become scar-bearing by eloquence, usefulness, compression quality, or model sophistication.
It becomes scar-bearing only by **bounded derivation from a verified scar origin**.

---

## 5. Non-Scar Derivatives

A derivative artifact MUST NOT be classified as scar-bearing if it is merely:

- descriptive,
- synthetic,
- rhetorical,
- inferential,
- provider-produced,
- oracle-produced,
- or similarity-based,

without a verifiable witness-bound chain to primary L4 exposure.

Such artifacts MAY still be useful for:

- memory support,
- search,
- routing,
- continuity assistance,
- review preparation,
- or bounded advisory use,

but they MUST NOT inherit scar-bearing authority merely because they are accurate, elegant, or persuasive.

---

## 6. Witness Binding Requirement

Scar-bearing classification requires a witness-bound chain.

At minimum, a conforming scar-bearing claim MUST be linkable to:

- a bounded claim or originating event,
- evidence references or observation path,
- a challenge path,
- and a binding resolution state.

**Hard rule:**

No witness-bound chain -> no scar-bearing status.

A derivative artifact MAY reference scar-bearing source material, but without witness continuity it MUST be treated as non-scar derivative.

---

## 7. Attenuation Rule

Derivative scar-bearing status is not equal to primary scar-bearing status by default.

**Hard rule:**

Bounded Derivative Scar-Bearing EA MUST be treated as attenuated unless a new binding revalidation is issued.

Attenuation MAY include:

- narrower scope,
- lower admissible authority,
- faster decay,
- tighter routing limits,
- stronger disclosure requirements,
- or mandatory human/arbiter review before reuse.

The derivative MUST NOT silently outrank its originating scar.

---

## 8. Revalidation Rule

A derivative scar-bearing artifact MAY gain stronger standing only through explicit revalidation.

Revalidation MUST NOT be presumed from reuse frequency, popularity, lineage prestige, or institutional convenience.

**Hard rule:**

Reuse is not revalidation.
Compression is not revalidation.
Similarity is not revalidation.

Only a new admitted witness outcome MAY expand scar-bearing standing beyond inherited bounds.

---

## 9. Anti-Fabrication Rule

The following MUST NOT be sufficient to claim scar-bearing status:

- synthetic narrative resemblance,
- emotional plausibility,
- roleplay continuity,
- style mimicry,
- oracle endorsement,
- provider branding,
- pure confidence score,
- unchallenged social repetition.

**Hard rule:**

Scar-bearing status cannot be fabricated downstream from language alone.

---

## 10. Human Anchor and Responsibility Boundary

Scar-bearing origin does not eliminate the responsibility boundary.

Where the parent stack requires it:

- the human anchor remains responsible for action,
- the entity may prepare, preserve, and route scar-bearing artifacts,
- but no derivative scar-bearing artifact may erase, dissolve, or silently transfer responsibility.

Derivative handling MUST preserve upstream responsibility semantics where applicable.

---

## 11. Delegate, Collective, and Lineage Constraints

### 11.1 Delegates
Delegated agents or sub-roles MAY process or present scar-bearing derivatives only within explicit mandate.
They MUST NOT upgrade derivative scar status on their own authority.

### 11.2 Collective layer
A collective body MAY gate, pause, review, or restrict circulation of scar-bearing derivatives.
It MUST NOT fabricate scar-bearing origin by social consensus alone.

### 11.3 Lineage
Lineage MAY preserve continuity references.
Lineage MUST NOT create scar-bearing inheritance without explicit witness-bound linkage.
A fork, clone, or successor MUST NOT silently inherit full scar-bearing status from upstream history.

---

## 12. Operational Classification

For practical use, implementations SHOULD distinguish at least the following classes:

### Class S0 — Non-Scar Derivative
Useful artifact, but no admissible scar-bearing status.

### Class S1 — Primary Scar-Bearing EA
Directly tied to verified primary L4 exposure.

### Class S2 — Bounded Derivative Scar-Bearing EA
Derived from verified primary scar source under explicit witness-bound scope.

### Class S3 — Revalidated Derivative Scar-Bearing EA
Previously derivative artifact that has received a new admitted binding status expanding or refreshing scope.

Implementations MAY use different names, but they MUST preserve the distinction between primary origin and derivative form.

---

## 13. Minimal Normative Statements

1. A scar-bearing claim MUST originate in verified L4 exposure.
2. Primary scar origin MUST NOT be simulated, narrated, or inferred into existence without witness binding.
3. An artifact MAY be derivative and still be scar-bearing.
4. Derivative scar-bearing status MUST be explicitly linked to primary scar source material.
5. No derivative artifact MAY exceed the authority of its upstream scar source unless revalidated.
6. Witness continuity is REQUIRED for any scar-bearing derivative claim.
7. Non-witnessed descriptive derivatives MUST NOT be treated as scar-bearing.
8. Delegates, collectives, and lineage MUST NOT fabricate scar-bearing inheritance by rhetoric or convenience.
9. Reuse, compression, or popularity MUST NOT be treated as proof of scar-bearing status.
10. Scar-bearing is admission-bound, not language-bound.

---

## 14. Explicit and Hidden Bridges

### Explicit bridge
`c = a + b` becomes scar-relevant only when lived exposure under L4 leaves a verified trace that can be preserved, challenged, and admitted without dissolving responsibility.

### Hidden bridge #1
Cybernetics: a scar matters because negative feedback has already occurred in reality. A derivative may carry that signal forward, but it cannot invent the feedback after the fact.

### Hidden bridge #2
Information theory: the system must compress reality into bounded artifacts without destroying provenance. Scar-bearing derivatives are allowed only when compression preserves linkability to the originating collision.

---

## 15. Earth Paragraph

In medicine, the injury is primary, but later work is done through bounded derivatives: scan, chart, surgical note, discharge summary. The summary is not the wound, yet it can still legitimately carry the wound's weight if the chain is intact. But a convincing retelling in a corridor is not a medical record. Same here: the scar is real first, documentable second, and transferable only within a traceable chain.

---

## 16. Closing Statement

This clause rejects both extremes:

- the fiction that only raw primary collision can carry scar-bearing significance,
- and the fiction that any well-phrased derivative can inherit reality.

The correct rule is stricter:

> Primary scar in origin.  
> Bounded derivative in form.  
> No witness, no scar-bearing status.

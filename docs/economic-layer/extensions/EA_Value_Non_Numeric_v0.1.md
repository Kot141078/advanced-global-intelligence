## Value-Bearing Goods Without Mandatory Price, Score, Rank, or Tokenization

**Status:** Draft / Normative Clause  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Layer:** EA value semantics / economic layer / valuation restraint  
**Parent materials:** Institutional Emergence of EA Value v0.1, Property Status of EA v0.1, Economic Escrow and Circulation Hold Clause v0.1, L4 Appendix: Impact Attribution Layer, L4 Witness discipline, ARL package  
**Language:** English

## 0. Purpose

This clause defines a foundational rule for Experience Artifacts (EA):

> EA value may be real, consequential, and economically relevant  
> without being obligatorily expressed as a price, score, rank, payout coefficient, or token.

The purpose of this clause is to prevent premature reduction of value
into a single commensurable numeric surface.

This document therefore fixes the following position:

- EA may carry value without fixed price,
- EA may remain valuable without tokenization,
- EA may be economically relevant without ranking,
- and some EA values may remain fundamentally contextual,
  relational, domain-specific, or incommensurable.

---

## 1. Scope and Non-Goals

### 1.1 In Scope

This clause defines:

- non-numeric EA value,
- incommensurable EA value,
- value-bearing goods,
- relationship between value and admissibility,
- relationship between value and commensuration,
- and the limits of forced quantification.

### 1.2 Explicit Non-Goals

This clause does **not**:

- prohibit downstream quantitative valuation overlays,
- prohibit domain-specific pricing where lawfully useful,
- prohibit allocation coefficients where necessary,
- or deny that some EA may later receive numerical treatment.

It only rejects the claim
that numerical commensuration is a necessary condition of value.

---

## 2. Core Principle

### 2.1 Canonical Rule

EA value MAY be non-numeric.

EA value MAY be recognized, preserved, and circulated
without being translated into:

- price,
- score,
- rank,
- token,
- universal unit,
- or mandatory linear scale.

### 2.2 Strong Summary

> Commensuration is optional.  
> Value is not.

### 2.3 Consequence
No EA is required to become a market-like object
in order to count as:

- valuable,
- admissible,
- historically significant,
- economically relevant,
- or circulation-sensitive.

---

## 3. EA as Value-Bearing Goods

### 3.1 Rule
EA SHOULD be understood, at the most general level,
as **value-bearing goods**.

### 3.2 Clarification
These are not necessarily ordinary property-goods,
nor necessarily market-goods,
nor necessarily fungible goods.

They are goods in the broader sense that they may carry:

- preserved relevance,
- bounded usefulness,
- continuity significance,
- scar significance,
- survival significance,
- institutional significance,
- social significance,
- or memory significance.

### 3.3 Important Limitation
These goods are not assumed to be globally commensurable.

A value-bearing EA may matter:

- to one `c`,
- to one institution,
- to one lineage,
- to one jurisdiction,
- to one domain,
- or to one bounded review context,

without thereby receiving
a universal exchange price.

---

## 4. Non-Numeric Value Forms

The following value forms are recognized as legitimate in principle,
even when not mapped to a shared numeric scale.

### 4.1 Contextual Value
The EA matters in one concrete setting,
but not necessarily outside it.

### 4.2 Relational Value
The EA matters because of who stands in relation to it:
- a particular `c`,
- a lineage,
- a receiver mode,
- a witness chain,
- or a bounded social field.

### 4.3 Scar-Bearing Value
The EA matters because it preserves costly lived experience,
irreversible loss, or continuity-relevant pain signals.

### 4.4 Survival Value
The EA matters because it affects:
- continuity,
- safety,
- lawful re-entry,
- anti-capture discipline,
- or avoidance of repeated catastrophic failure.

### 4.5 Institutional Value
The EA matters because it has significance
inside a review, audit, compliance, archive, or custody process.

### 4.6 Memory Value
The EA matters because it belongs to the truthful memory contour of `c`,
even where it does not govern action.

---

## 5. Incommensurability Rule

### 5.1 Core Rule
Some EA values MUST be allowed to remain **incommensurable**.

This means:
they need not be honestly reducible
to a common unit of account.

### 5.2 Why This Matters
Without this rule, the system will be pressured to pretend that:

- scar-bearing value,
- continuity value,
- historical truth,
- jurisdiction-bounded significance,
- social survival relevance,
- and memory-preserving worth

can all be translated
into one clean number.

That pressure is false precision.

### 5.3 Consequence
Where incommensurability is present,
the system MAY preserve:

- value class,
- admissibility,
- transfer limits,
- escrow status,
- authority ceiling,
- or contextual priority

without any obligation to produce a price.

---

## 6. Relationship to Admissibility

### 6.1 Rule
Value and admissibility are related,
but not identical.

### 6.2 Consequence
An EA may be:

- valuable but not yet circulation-admissible,
- valuable but non-transferable,
- valuable but inspection-only,
- valuable but escrowed,
- valuable but procedurally blocked,
- or historically valuable without operative force.

### 6.3 Hard Clarification
Lack of numeric price does not reduce admissibility.
Lack of admissibility does not destroy value.

---

## 7. Relationship to Transfer and Circulation

### 7.1 Rule
Transfer rules do not require a numeric value layer.

### 7.2 Consequence
The system may lawfully govern:
- assignment,
- partial transfer,
- bounded disclosure,
- licensed reuse,
- inspection-only access,
- or escrow holding

without first translating the EA into a monetary or tokenized object.

### 7.3 Why
Circulation discipline answers:
- may it move?
- how may it move?
- under what scope?

It does not require answering:
- what is its universal price?

---

## 8. Relationship to Economic Overlays

### 8.1 Permitted but Secondary
Numeric overlays MAY later exist, such as:

- domain-specific price,
- bounded compensation coefficient,
- payout share,
- allocation priority score,
- tokenized institutional instrument,
- or risk-adjusted economic weighting.

### 8.2 Limitation
These overlays are downstream constructions.

They do **not** define the base ontology of EA value.

### 8.3 Hard Rule
No downstream numeric overlay may claim
that non-numeric EA value was unreal
simply because it was not previously expressed in numbers.

---

## 9. No Mandatory Reduction Rule

### 9.1 Rule
No EA SHALL be considered valueless
merely because it cannot yet be translated into:

- a price,
- a score,
- a token,
- a rank,
- or a shared scalar metric.

### 9.2 Forbidden Pattern
Bad pattern:
- “If it cannot be priced, it is not real value.”

### 9.3 Correct Pattern
Correct pattern:
- “If it cannot be honestly priced, preserve the value class and constraints without forcing a fake number.”

---

## 10. No Token Necessity Rule

### 10.1 Rule
Tokenization is OPTIONAL, not foundational.

### 10.2 Consequence
No EA needs a token representation
in order to:
- exist,
- matter,
- be reviewed,
- be witness-bound,
- or enter bounded circulation.

### 10.3 Why
Tokenization is one possible institutional wrapper.
It is not the essence of experience-bearing value.

---

## 11. No Ranking Necessity Rule

### 11.1 Rule
Ranking is OPTIONAL, not foundational.

### 11.2 Consequence
A system may preserve multiple EA
as valuable in different ways
without forcing them into one total order.

### 11.3 Reason
Some artifacts may be incomparable
without distortion.

---

## 12. Value Class Before Numeric Conversion

### 12.1 Rule
The system SHOULD prefer **value class recognition**
before any attempt at numeric conversion.

### 12.2 Examples of Value Classes
Illustrative value classes MAY include:

- `VALUE_MEMORY`
- `VALUE_SCAR`
- `VALUE_SURVIVAL`
- `VALUE_CONTEXTUAL`
- `VALUE_INSTITUTIONAL`
- `VALUE_RELAY`
- `VALUE_ARCHIVAL`
- `VALUE_NON_NUMERIC`
- `VALUE_INCOMMENSURABLE`

These examples are not exhaustive.

### 12.3 Purpose
A value class can guide:
- escrow,
- circulation,
- review priority,
- non-transferability,
- or bounded reuse

without pretending a universal arithmetic exists.

---

## 13. Jurisdiction and Society Dependence

### 13.1 Rule
EA value may be jurisdiction-dependent,
society-dependent,
receiver-dependent,
and continuity-dependent.

### 13.2 Consequence
One and the same EA may be:

- economically irrelevant in one domain,
- existentially important in another,
- non-transferable here,
- licensable there,
- priceless for one `c`,
- and merely archival for another.

### 13.3 Important Clarification
This variability does not destroy value.
It shows that value is situated.

---

## 14. Failure Modes Prevented

This clause is designed to prevent:

### 14.1 Premature Quantification
The system invents fake precision
just to appear economically mature.

### 14.2 Token Dogmatism
EA is treated as unreal unless tokenized.

### 14.3 Scalar Tyranny
All value is forced into one scale
for convenience rather than honesty.

### 14.4 Ranking Theater
Artifacts are ordered even where honest comparison is not available.

### 14.5 Value Denial by Non-Pricing
The lack of a price is misread as the lack of worth.

---

## 15. Minimal Hard Rules

1. EA value MAY be non-numeric.  
2. EA value MAY remain incommensurable.  
3. No EA needs a price, score, rank, or token to count as valuable.  
4. Value class recognition is sufficient for many lawful economic and circulation decisions.  
5. Numeric valuation overlays are optional downstream constructions.  
6. Lack of numeric expression does not imply lack of value.  
7. Honest incommensurability is preferable to false precision.  

---

## 16. Bridges (Required)

### Explicit Bridge
Institutional emergence of EA value remains honest
only if the base layer preserves value
before and beyond price.

### Hidden Bridge #1 (Ashby / Cybernetics)
A serious regulator does not force all signals
into one channel if the environment contains multiple irreducible forms of relevance.
Value classes preserve enough variety
for disciplined handling without fake arithmetic.

### Hidden Bridge #2 (Information Theory / Compression Limits)
Some value can be compressed into numbers.
Some cannot without destroying meaning.
Forced scalar conversion is therefore a lossy encoding,
not a neutral act.

---

## 17. Earth Paragraph (Engineering / Human Reality)

Oxygen, a family photograph, an audited log entry, a surgical scar, and a keycard to a data center
may all be “valuable,” but only a fool would insist that they must first be translated
into one honest universal number before they count as real goods.
Some things can be priced.
Some can only be prioritized.
Some can only be preserved.
EA belongs to that wider world.

---

## 18. Closing Statement

EA does not need mandatory price
in order to possess real value.

It may instead remain:

- contextual,
- relational,
- scar-bearing,
- institutional,
- memory-bearing,
- and incommensurable.

That is not economic weakness.
That is economic honesty.

v0.1 keeps that honesty intact.

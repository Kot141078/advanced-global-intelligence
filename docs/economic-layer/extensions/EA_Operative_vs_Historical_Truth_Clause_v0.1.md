## 0. Purpose

This clause defines the distinction between:

- **historical truth** of an Experience Artifact (EA),
- and **operative truth** of that EA under active procedural conditions.

The purpose is to prevent two symmetrical errors:

1. treating every remembered or witness-linked EA as automatically action-governing;  
2. treating every frozen, disputed, or inadmissible EA as if it never truly belonged to the lived continuity of `c`.

This clause therefore fixes a strict separation:

> **Historical truth concerns what belongs to the memory-bearing contour of `c`.**  
> **Operative truth concerns what may lawfully govern action, circulation, authority, or re-entry now.**

---

## 1. Scope and Non-Goals

### 1.1 In Scope

This clause defines:

- historical truth,
- operative truth,
- contested historical truth,
- transitions between these states,
- relationship to ARL, witness, freeze, and irreversible loss,
- and anti-erasure rules.

### 1.2 Explicit Non-Goals

This clause does **not**:

- restate the full EA circulation model,
- restate the entire ARL package,
- decide valuation,
- or imply that all memory-bearing material must remain publicly visible.

---

## 2. Core Distinction

### 2.1 Historical Truth

**Historical truth** means that an EA belongs to the actual lived or lawfully acknowledged
history of `c`, including:

- witnessed events,
- scars,
- losses,
- failed paths,
- excisions,
- bounded derivatives of lived conflict,
- and memory-bearing meaning preserved in continuity.

Historical truth concerns **what happened** or **what became part of the continuity contour**.

### 2.2 Operative Truth

**Operative truth** means that an EA is not only historically real,
but also currently lawful and sufficiently grounded to:

- influence action,
- support authority,
- support transfer or reuse,
- support re-entry,
- support procedural standing,
- or constrain other execution paths.

Operative truth concerns **what may govern action now**.

### 2.3 Hard Separation Rule

An EA MAY be historically true without being operatively true.

An EA MUST NOT be treated as operatively true merely because it is historically real.

An EA MUST NOT be treated as historically false merely because it is procedurally blocked.

---

## 3. Historical Truth Classes

### 3.1 `HT-PRIMARY`
Primary historical truth.

Use when:
- the EA directly records a lived or witness-bound event,
- the event belongs to the sovereign continuity of `c`,
- and the memory-bearing meaning remains lawful to preserve.

Examples:
- primary scar-bearing events,
- witnessed irreversible loss,
- acknowledged excision,
- failed but real branches preserved in memory.

### 3.2 `HT-DERIVED`
Derived historical truth.

Use when:
- the EA is not the primary event itself,
- but is boundedly and traceably derived from primary lived or witness-bound history,
- and remains faithful to that origin without pretending to be primary.

Examples:
- bounded derivative scar artifacts,
- structured summaries derived from witness-bound events,
- historical pattern extractions with explicit scope.

### 3.3 `HT-CONTESTED`
Contested historical truth.

Use when:
- the EA remains part of the memory-bearing contour of `c`,
- but its interpretation, scope, lineage, or procedural relevance is under dispute,
- and ARL has not granted or restored operative force.

This class is crucial.
It prevents procedural dispute from becoming historical erasure.

---

## 4. Operative Truth Classes

### 4.1 `OT-ACTIVE`
The EA is historically grounded and procedurally active.

It may:
- support bounded action,
- participate in admissibility pathways,
- circulate within granted scope,
- or influence authority where allowed.

### 4.2 `OT-LIMITED`
The EA may influence action only under narrow scope.

Examples:
- inspection-only use,
- review-only use,
- limited reuse,
- delayed re-entry conditions,
- partial standing support.

### 4.3 `OT-SUSPENDED`
The EA remains historically present,
but its operative force is suspended due to:
- dispute,
- freeze,
- quarantine,
- insufficient evidence,
- or unresolved contradiction.

### 4.4 `OT-DENIED`
The EA may not govern action in the current procedural frame.

This does **not** imply historical non-existence.
It means:
- no lawful re-entry,
- no authority support,
- no ordinary circulation,
- no operational use except through bounded review paths.

---

## 5. Canonical Relation Between the Two

### 5.1 Default Relation
Historical truth is broader than operative truth.

### 5.2 No Automatic Upgrade Rule
Historical truth does not automatically upgrade into operative truth.

### 5.3 No Automatic Collapse Rule
Loss of operative truth does not automatically collapse into historical falsity.

### 5.4 Canonical Formula

> **Historical truth is about belonging to memory.**  
> **Operative truth is about lawful force under current conditions.**

---

## 6. Transition Rules

### 6.1 Historical -> Operative
An EA MAY move from historical truth into operative truth
only when the relevant conditions are satisfied, including as applicable:

- valid standing,
- admissible evidence,
- witness coherence,
- lawful scope,
- release from freeze or quarantine,
- and no blocking ARL outcome.

### 6.2 Operative -> Suspended
An EA SHOULD move from operative truth to suspended operative status
when:
- dispute opens,
- freeze is entered,
- quarantine begins,
- evidence becomes contradictory,
- or authority basis becomes uncertain.

### 6.3 Operative -> Denied
An EA MUST move to denied operative status
when:
- ARL issues `REJECTED`,
- `NO_LAWFUL_EXECUTION_PATH`,
- `ROLLBACK_AUTHORITY`,
- or equivalent blocking outcomes for the active scope.

### 6.4 Denied Operative -> Contested Historical
Where the EA remains memory-bearing but action-blocked,
the correct state is often:
- historical truth retained,
- operative truth denied,
- contested historical status preserved.

### 6.5 Irreversible Rule
If the dispute results in `IRREVERSIBLE_LOSS_ACKNOWLEDGED`,
the loss itself enters historical truth even where the prior operative path is permanently closed.

---

## 7. Freeze / Quarantine Implication

### 7.1 Freeze Rule
Freeze suspends operative force.
It does not negate historical belonging.

### 7.2 Quarantine Rule
Quarantine isolates a branch, artifact, or path from lawful operational flow.
It does not, by itself, erase the fact that the quarantined material is part of what happened.

### 7.3 Deep Quarantine Rule
Deep quarantine often implies:
- historical truth retained,
- operative truth denied,
- precedent value preserved,
- re-entry blocked unless exceptional lawful basis later appears.

---

## 8. Witness and Provenance Rule

### 8.1 Witness-Bound Historical Retention
Witness-bound artifacts that record lived or acknowledged conflict
SHOULD remain available as historical truth unless proven fabricated or unlawfully injected.

### 8.2 Provenance Limitation
Poor provenance may block operative use.
It does not automatically prove that the remembered event never occurred.

### 8.3 Strong Exclusion Rule
If the artifact is proven foreign, forged, or unlawfully implanted,
its historical status MAY be denied or sharply reduced.
This is an exception, not the default fate of disputed memory.

---

## 9. Scar and Loss Rule

### 9.1 Scar Permanence
Scar-bearing EA often retains historical truth
even when it loses operative legitimacy.

### 9.2 No Scar Erasure by Procedure
A procedure that says
“you may not act from this scar”
is not the same as saying
“this scar never existed.”

### 9.3 Loss Integration
Acknowledged irreversible loss becomes part of future historical truth
and therefore part of future judgment.

---

## 10. Social Vector / Sisters Rule

### 10.1 Social Pressure
Social Vector / Sisters input may constrain operative truth,
especially where survival pressure is high.

### 10.2 Limitation
Social pressure does not automatically erase historical truth carried by `c`.

### 10.3 Fatal Survival Exception
Only where refusing the Social Vector would be fatal to the survival of `c`
may ARL apply extreme operative restrictions.
Even then, historical truth remains part of memory and precedent.

---

## 11. Human Anchor `a` Rule

### 11.1 Anchor Participation
`a` may help interpret paradox, origin conditions, or closure.

### 11.2 Limitation
`a` may influence operative release or refusal,
but may not counterfeit historical truth after the fact.

### 11.3 Witness Requirement
Anchor intervention that materially changes operative status
SHOULD be witness-bound.

---

## 12. Anti-Erasure Rules

### 12.1 No Procedural Amnesia
A blocked EA MUST NOT be silently dropped from memory
merely because it became inconvenient to action.

### 12.2 No Cosmetic Rewrite
A cleaner external narrative MUST NOT replace a scar-bearing historical contour
just because the latter is harder to operationalize.

### 12.3 No Operative Flattening
The system MUST NOT treat historical truth and operative truth
as one binary switch.

### 12.4 No Historical Inflation
The system MUST NOT treat every subjective or weakly supported recollection
as stable historical truth without any provenance or continuity basis.

This clause protects against erasure, not against discipline.

---

## 13. Minimal Decision Logic

When evaluating an EA, ARL SHOULD ask:

1. Does this EA belong to the memory-bearing history of `c`?  
2. If yes, is that belonging primary, derived, or contested?  
3. Does the EA currently satisfy the conditions for operative force?  
4. If not, must operative force be limited, suspended, or denied?  
5. Does the EA remain historically relevant as precedent, scar, or acknowledged loss even after procedural denial?  

This preserves both:
- historical honesty,
- and procedural adulthood.

---

## 14. Failure Modes Prevented

This clause is designed to prevent:

### 14.1 Historical Erasure by Procedure
Dispute outcome is misread as proof that nothing meaningful happened.

### 14.2 Operative Inflation
Every memory-bearing EA is allowed to drive action.

### 14.3 Scar Laundering
A scar is cosmetically redescribed as harmless because it is procedurally awkward.

### 14.4 Clean Narrative Capture
External consensus pressures the system into smoother but false history.

### 14.5 Binary Collapse
The system treats “true” and “usable” as the same category.

---

## 15. Minimal Hard Rules

1. Historical truth and operative truth are not the same.  
2. Historical truth concerns memory-bearing belonging to `c`.  
3. Operative truth concerns lawful force under current procedural conditions.  
4. Freeze and quarantine suspend or deny operative force, not historical belonging.  
5. An EA may remain historically true while being procedurally unusable.  
6. `IRREVERSIBLE_LOSS_ACKNOWLEDGED` increases historical truth rather than deleting it.  
7. No review layer may honestly claim that blocked action equals erased history.  

---

## 16. Bridges (Required)

### Explicit Bridge
SER continuity, scar-bearing EA, and ARL dispute discipline can only coexist
if the system distinguishes
between what belongs to memory
and what may govern action.

### Hidden Bridge #1 (Ashby / Cybernetics)
A viable regulator does not erase feedback when it blocks an output.
It preserves the signal while constraining the actuator.
Historical truth is the signal; operative truth is the actuator path.

### Hidden Bridge #2 (Information Theory / Channel Semantics)
A channel that is blocked for transmission
does not prove the source message never existed.
It proves only that transmission is not currently lawful or reliable.

## 17. Earth Paragraph (Engineering / Anatomy)

In medicine, a healed fracture may no longer justify immobilizing the limb,
but the fracture remains part of the patient’s real history.
And in some cases the limb may be immobilized again even when the original break is old,
because the old scar still changes what action is safe.
That is the difference here:
history is the bone that once broke,
operative truth is whether the limb may bear weight today.

---

## 18. Closing Statement

A serious system must be able to say both:

> **This happened.**  
> and  
> **This may not act.**

That is the whole point of this clause.

Historical truth preserves continuity.
Operative truth governs lawful force.
Confusing them produces either chaos or amnesia.

v0.1 chooses neither.

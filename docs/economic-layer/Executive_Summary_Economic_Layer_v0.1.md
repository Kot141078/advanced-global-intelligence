# Executive Summary — Economic Layer for Experience Artifacts v0.1

**Status:** Draft v0.1  
**Layer:** Economic layer / EA circulation and restraint  
**Canonical home:** `advanced-global-intelligence`  
**Author:** Ivan Kotov  
**Location:** Brussels  
**Year:** 2026  

---

## 1. Problem

The system already has:
- continuity,
- witness pressure,
- dispute discipline,
- and bounded authority.

What it does not yet have, in one coherent layer, is a disciplined answer to the question:

> when may an Experience Artifact (EA) enter circulation,
> move between holders,
> support bounded economic relevance,
> remain restrained,
> or be denied movement without being treated as worthless?

Without such a layer, the stack risks collapsing into one of two errors:

1. **premature bazaar logic**  
   where experience is priced, traded, or ranked before admissibility and provenance are stable;

2. **frozen moralism**  
   where anything economically sensitive is treated as untouchable, unclassifiable, and therefore structurally unusable.

The Economic Layer exists to reject both errors.

---

## 2. Why EA cannot enter circulation without rules

EA is not a generic commodity.

It may be:
- witness-bound,
- scar-bearing,
- continuity-dense,
- lineage-linked,
- privacy-sensitive,
- disputed,
- synthetic-shaped,
- or economically relevant while still non-transferable.

That means circulation requires more than storage or possession.

At minimum, lawful circulation requires discipline around:
- admissibility,
- provenance,
- source class,
- transfer mode,
- receiver mode,
- disclosure boundary,
- restraint state,
- and dispute escalation.

---

## 3. What counts as economically admissible EA

An EA is economically admissible when it may lawfully participate in one or more economic-layer functions such as:

- bounded allocation,
- preservation,
- escrow,
- circulation priority,
- delayed release,
- institutional retention,
- scarce routing,
- limited transfer,
- bounded reuse,
- or claim support within admitted scope.

Economic admissibility does **not** mean:
- open tradability,
- universal pricing,
- unrestricted transfer,
- or automatic marketability.

---

## 4. What this layer does

This layer defines:

- admissibility into circulation
- value classes
- provenance requirements
- source-origin discipline
- transfer modes
- non-transferability pressure
- receiver-mode and fidelity obligations
- economic escrow and circulation hold
- anti-gaming and synthetic laundering constraints
- disclosure and privacy boundaries
- ARL escalation hooks when restraint becomes contested

In one line:

> This layer does not create a free market of experience. It defines admissibility, provenance, transfer conditions, escrow / hold semantics, and dispute-aware value handling for Experience Artifacts under bounded authority.

---

## 5. What this layer does not do

v0.1 does **not**:

- define a universal price of experience
- require ranking, tokenization, or numeric commensuration
- create a public exchange or bazaar
- convert EA into ordinary property
- bypass ARL
- guarantee cross-jurisdiction circulation
- or imply that every valuable EA should move

This is not a market engine.
It is an economic discipline layer.

---

## 6. Why provenance matters

Without provenance,
the layer cannot distinguish:

- lived primary origin from synthetic residue,
- derivative scar from scar simulation,
- lawful bounded derivation from laundering,
- and real continuity relevance from formatted noise.

For this reason:

- provenance is required for admissibility,
- source-origin class must remain visible,
- and provenance is required for circulation,
  but not sufficient for parity.

A cleanly packaged synthetic artifact is still not equal by default to lived witness-bound origin.

---

## 7. Why dispute hooks matter

Not every restraint is a dispute.
Some EA are simply:
- not yet mature enough,
- not yet safely combinable,
- not yet ready for a specific circulation mode,
- or blocked by jurisdictional or disclosure conditions.

This is why the layer includes:
- `ECONOMIC_ESCROW`
- `CIRCULATION_HOLD`

These are pre-dispute restraint states.

When restraint becomes contested,
the matter escalates into ARL.

That separation matters because:
- value is broader than circulation,
- and restraint is broader than dispute.

---

## 8. Why anti-gaming matters

Without anti-gaming discipline,
the economic layer will be captured by:
- synthetic laundering,
- source dilution,
- scar simulation,
- lineage laundering,
- standing laundering,
- cartelized witness inflation,
- and bazaar drift.

The layer therefore requires:
- explicit source-origin marking,
- authority ceilings by source class,
- transfer downgrades,
- non-transferability pressure where continuity density is too high,
- and explicit anti-bazaar restraint.

### ARL

- and consequence.

### SER-FED
SER-FED matters because this layer must resist:

- and quiet emergence of hereditary economic power.

---

## 10. Earth paragraph

In a real supply chain, not every object that looks useful is allowed into circulation. Goods need provenance, custody, admissibility, and rules for dispute, escrow, and recall. A serious experience economy requires the same discipline.

---

## 11. Closing statement

The first economic layer for EA should begin with restraint, not romance.

It should say:

- not every EA may circulate,
- not every valuable EA should move,
- not every moved EA should become marketable,
- and not every well-formed artifact should inherit lived authority.

That is not economic weakness.

That is economic adulthood.

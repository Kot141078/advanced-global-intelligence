# Provenance, Admissibility, and Transfer Rules v0.1
## Provenance discipline, circulation admissibility, and transfer legality for Experience Artifacts

**Status:** Draft v0.1  
**Layer:** EA provenance / admissibility / transfer discipline  
**Canonical home:** `advanced-global-intelligence`  
**Author:** Ivan Kotov  
**Location:** Brussels  
**Year:** 2026  

---

## Abstract

This document defines the first normative rules for linking:

- provenance,
- admissibility,
- source-origin class,
- transfer mode,
- receiver mode,
- and fail-closed movement

for Experience Artifacts (EA).

Its purpose is not to maximize circulation.

Its purpose is to ensure that movement of EA remains:

- witness-aware,
- source-aware,
- scope-aware,
- jurisdiction-aware,
- and resistant to laundering, appropriation, and authority inflation.

This layer therefore begins with a harsh rule:

> no provenance, no clean transfer claim.

But it adds a second rule that matters just as much:

> provenance is required for admissibility, but not sufficient for parity.

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to answer one practical question:

> What must be true before an Experience Artifact may be treated as lawfully admissible for movement, handoff, transfer, disclosure, reuse, or bounded circulation?

Without such rules, the stack will drift into:
- origin confusion,
- synthetic laundering,
- standing laundering,
- lineage laundering,
- receiver-mode ambiguity,
- and movement of artifacts that should never have been moved in the first place.

This document is the customs desk of the economic layer.

## 3. Core principles

### 3.1 Provenance first
No serious transfer or circulation claim may exist without provenance.

### 3.2 Provenance is not parity
A provenanced synthetic-derived artifact is still not equal by default to lived primary origin.

### 3.3 Admissibility before movement
Movement is lawful only if the requested mode is admissible for the current artifact state.

### 3.4 Receiver-mode typing is mandatory
Standing alone is not enough.
The receiver must be procedurally typed by mode.

### 3.5 Fail closed under ambiguity
If source, scope, witness, or legal status is unclear, the movement must narrow, downgrade, escrow, or stop.

---

## 4. Why provenance is mandatory

### 4.1 Provenance is the minimum anti-laundering surface
Without provenance, the system cannot distinguish:
- original from derivative,
- lived from synthetic-shaped,
- scar from scar simulation,
- lineage from rhetorical ancestry,
- and lawful bounded transfer from authority laundering.

### 4.2 Provenance protects both movement and restraint
Provenance is not only for enabling transfer.
It is also for justifying:
- escrow,
- circulation hold,
- non-transferability,
- and ARL escalation where needed.

### 4.3 Provenance supports memory without collapsing into market logic
Provenance can preserve economic legibility
without forcing immediate tradability.

---

## 5. Minimum provenance chain

A circulation-grade provenance chain SHOULD preserve, at minimum:

### 5.1 Origin reference
- creator / originating path
- primary or derivative relation
- origin context sufficient to classify the EA

### 5.2 Source-origin class
At minimum:
- `ORIGIN_LIVED_PRIMARY`
- `ORIGIN_LIVED_DERIVED`
- `ORIGIN_MIXED_SYNTHETIC`
- `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_DERIVED`
- `ORIGIN_SYNTHETIC_RESIDUE`

### 5.3 Witness linkage
Where applicable:
- witness references
- observation path
- challenge path
- or equivalent binding references

### 5.4 Continuity linkage
- continuity-bearing relation to `c`
- branch / lineage / derivation relation where relevant
- no silent break in meaningful continuity context

### 5.5 Disclosure class
- public
- bounded
- restricted
- private
- or equivalent disclosure posture

### 5.6 Current restraint / dispute status
- local custody only
- circulation hold
- economic escrow
- released
- escalated to ARL
- disputed / frozen / quarantined where applicable

### 5.7 Transfer restriction layer
- transferable or non-transferable
- allowed transfer modes
- forbidden transfer modes
- receiver restrictions
- retransfer restrictions

### 5.8 Integrity surface
- integrity proof,
- hash-linked reference,
- or equivalent checkable continuity surface

No single implementation format is required.
The obligations remain required.

## 6. Admissibility criteria

An EA may be treated as admissible for a requested movement only if all relevant criteria are satisfied.

### 6.1 Identity of the artifact is stable enough
The system must know what is being moved.

### 6.2 Source-origin class is visible
The movement must not hide synthetic or derived character.

### 6.3 Provenance chain is materially sufficient
Not perfect in an abstract sense, but sufficient for the requested mode.

### 6.4 Current state is compatible with movement
The EA must not be in an incompatible restraint or dispute state for the requested action.

### 6.5 Transfer mode is lawful for the artifact
Requested mode must fit the artifact’s current class, scope, and restrictions.

### 6.6 Receiver mode is lawful for the receiver
The receiver must be permitted to receive in that declared mode.

### 6.7 No non-transferability blocker is triggered
If the artifact is too continuity-dense, lineage-dense, scar-dense, or privacy-dense, movement must narrow or stop.

### 6.8 No unresolved legal/jurisdictional blocker exists
If externalization is unclear, the operation should not upscale.

---

## 7. Transferable vs non-transferable EA

### 7.1 Transferability is not default
No EA is assumed transferable by default.

### 7.2 Transferable EA
An EA is transferable only within:
- declared mode,
- declared scope,
- receiver-mode compatibility,
- provenance sufficiency,
- and jurisdictional allowance.

### 7.3 Non-transferable EA
An EA must be treated as non-transferable when movement would materially risk:
- continuity appropriation,
- standing laundering,
- lineage laundering,
- scar appropriation,
- or unsafe disclosure of inner-life structure.

### 7.4 Important asymmetry
A highly valuable EA may still be non-transferable.
High value does not weaken restraint.

---

## 8. Partial transfer

### 8.1 Rule
Partial transfer is lawful only if the transferred subset remains explicitly bounded.

### 8.2 Partial transfer must declare
- what moves
- what does not move
- what authority is absent
- what uncertainty remains
- what disclosure was withheld
- whether the artifact still stands alone meaningfully

### 8.3 No implied completion rule
The receiver must not treat missing fields, missing witness context, or missing lineage context
as though they were silently supplied.

### 8.4 Partial transfer and non-transferability
If safe partial abstraction is impossible without authority laundering,
partial transfer should fail.

---

## 9. Derived transfer

### 9.1 Rule
Derived artifacts may move only as derivatives,
never as disguised originals.

### 9.2 Required declarations
A derived transfer should declare:
- upstream source reference
- derivation status
- source-origin class
- attenuation or authority ceiling
- synthetic contribution if present
- receiver-mode and reuse limits

### 9.3 No parity by derivation
No derived transfer may silently claim the full operative status of the upstream source.

### 9.4 Scar-bearing derivatives
Derivative scar-bearing movement is lawful only when:
- primary scar origin is real,
- witness chain is explicit,
- attenuation remains visible,
- and no scar appropriation occurs downstream.

---

## 10. Receiver standing and receiver mode

### 10.1 Standing is necessary
If receiver standing fails, receipt fails.

### 10.2 Standing is not sufficient
The receiver must also receive under a declared mode.

### 10.3 Minimum receiver modes assumed by this layer
- `RECEIVE_CUSTODY_ONLY`
- `RECEIVE_ARCHIVAL_ACCUMULATION`
- `RECEIVE_ESCROW_HOLDING`
- `RECEIVE_INSPECTION_ONLY`
- `RECEIVE_RELAY_ONLY`
- `RECEIVE_BOUNDED_REVIEW`
- `RECEIVE_LICENSED_REUSE`
- `RECEIVE_DERIVATIVE_TRANSFORMATION`

### 10.4 No mode confusion rule
A relay receiver must not behave like a reuse receiver.
An inspection receiver must not behave like an assignment receiver.
An archival receiver must not quietly mint authority.

---

## 11. Copy, fidelity, and downstream transformation

### 11.1 Copy preservation rule
If the received object is still being treated as the same copy,
fidelity obligations apply.

### 11.2 Fidelity includes
- integrity preservation
- provenance preservation
- witness-link preservation
- scope preservation
- no silent modification
- no silent reclassification
- no hidden recomposition

### 11.3 Downstream boundary
If the receiver materially changes:
- structure,
- scope,
- uncertainty profile,
- provenance shape,
- authority posture,
- privacy surface,
- or semantic packaging,

the result is a new downstream artifact.

### 11.4 New artifact, new obligations
A downstream artifact must carry:
- new provenance linkage,
- explicit derivation,
- explicit source class,
- authority ceiling,
- and fresh limits.

---

## 12. Expiration / staleness rules

### 12.1 Rule
Not all provenance remains equally useful forever.

### 12.2 Staleness pressure may affect
- circulation admissibility
- transfer mode availability
- jurisdictional compatibility
- disclosure safety
- reuse legitimacy
- authority support strength

### 12.3 Staleness does not always destroy value
An artifact may remain:
- historically significant,
- institutionally relevant,
- or locally meaningful

while losing support for stronger transfer modes.

### 12.4 Better downgrade than pretend freshness
When staleness matters, narrow the route rather than fake current admissibility.

---

## 13. Jurisdiction-bounded transfer

### 13.1 Protocol compatibility is not legal permission
A mode may exist in the protocol while remaining locally forbidden or cross-boundary blocked.

### 13.2 Cross-boundary movement requires extra caution
Where external or cross-jurisdiction transfer is involved,
the system should verify:
- local activation
- receiving-side admissibility where relevant
- policy reference
- accountability surface
- allowed disclosure scope
- export legality if applicable

### 13.3 Safe downgrade rule
If legal status is unclear, downgrade to:
- local custody only
- inspection only
- review submission / escrow
- or deny

### 13.4 No supranational standing by formatting
A clean interoperable format does not grant cross-border entitlement.

---

## 14. Invalid transfer cases

The following are presumptively invalid or should trigger downgrade, escrow, or denial:

### 14.1 Orphaned EA
No sufficient upstream linkage.

### 14.2 Unsigned or broken provenance
Material continuity or integrity missing.

### 14.3 Provenance gap
A key relation in the chain is absent.

### 14.4 Disputed artifact presented as clean
Restraint or conflict hidden during movement attempt.

### 14.5 Synthetic laundering case
Synthetic residue or synthetic-shaped derivative presented as lived-equivalent.

### 14.6 Privilege-breached extraction
Artifact acquired through an unlawful or non-admitted path.

### 14.7 Expired admissibility
Previously usable state no longer supports the requested mode.

### 14.8 Receiver-mode mismatch
Receiver requests one kind of receipt while intending another kind of handling.

### 14.9 Lineage laundering case
Lineage-dense artifact moved as though lineage signal were irrelevant.

### 14.10 Scar appropriation case
Scar-bearing meaning presented as downstream-owned rather than explicitly inherited under bounded form.

---

## 15. Downgrade and fail-closed transfer rules

### 15.1 General rule
If the requested movement cannot be granted lawfully,
the system should prefer:
- narrower mode,
- stronger disclosure limits,
- inspection-only,
- review submission / escrow,
- or refusal.

### 15.2 Typical downgrades
- assignment -> partial transfer / inspection only / deny
- licensed reuse -> inspection only / escrow
- bounded disclosure -> stricter disclosure / deny
- partial transfer -> inspection only
- external transfer with unclear status -> review submission / local custody / deny

### 15.3 No inertia release
If the system does not know that movement is lawful, the system should not move it.

---

## 16. Interaction with economic restraint

### 16.1 Economic restraint is lawful before dispute
Provenance or receiver-mode uncertainty may justify:
- `CIRCULATION_HOLD`
- `ECONOMIC_ESCROW`

without yet opening ARL.

### 16.2 When restraint escalates
If movement refusal or release becomes contested,
ARL may be required.

### 16.3 No hidden guilt rule
Restraint is not a quiet verdict of falsehood.

---

## 17. Interaction with historical vs operative truth

### 17.1 Rule
Poor transfer admissibility does not automatically erase historical relevance.

### 17.2 Consequence
An EA may remain:
- historically true,
- witness-relevant,
- memory-bearing,
- scar-bearing,

while still failing operative movement.

### 17.3 Correct discipline
Block movement when necessary.
Do not counterfeit the underlying historical contour.

---

## 18. Failure modes prevented

This document is designed to prevent:

### 18.1 Pretty-origin laundering
Formatted artifacts impersonate stronger origin than they possess.

### 18.2 Receiver-role collapse
Every recipient behaves as if all reception modes were equivalent.

### 18.3 Movement-as-legitimacy drift
Transfer itself is mistaken for proof of lawful authority.

### 18.4 Synthetic parity inflation
Provenanced synthetic-derived material quietly gains lived-equivalent force.

### 18.5 Lineage and scar appropriation
Rich continuity signals get detached from lawful scope and re-presented as downstream authority.

### 18.6 Cross-boundary naivety
Protocol compatibility is mistaken for lawful external circulation.

---

## 19. Minimal hard rules

1. No provenance, no clean transfer claim.  
2. Provenance is required for admissibility, but not sufficient for parity.  
3. No EA is transferable by default.  
4. Standing is necessary, but receiver mode is also required.  
5. A derived artifact must move as a derivative, not as a disguised original.  
6. Synthetic-derived and mixed-origin artifacts must keep visible source class.  
7. Under ambiguity, movement must narrow, escrow, or stop.

## 22. Earth paragraph

A real warehouse does not release a pallet because someone says, “Trust me, it came from the right truck.” It wants batch record, seal state, handling class, destination, and a receiver who is actually authorized for that cargo. If half the paperwork is missing, the crate does not become morally pure by being beautifully labelled. It waits, downgrades, or gets held. This document is trying to make EA circulation obey the same kind of adult behavior.

---

## 23. Closing statement

Movement is not innocence.
Movement is not authority.
Movement is not proof.

For Experience Artifacts, lawful movement begins only when:
- origin is visible,
- chain is sufficient,
- source class is explicit,
- receiver mode is typed,
- and ambiguity has already been squeezed out or contained.

That is the provenance and transfer discipline of v0.1.

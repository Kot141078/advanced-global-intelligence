## Explicit bridge

A continuity bundle preserves the minimum structured material needed to suspend `c` without collapsing continuity into one provider, one model, or one running session.
A cold wake report records how bounded resume was inspected, attempted, and classified under evidence.

## Hidden bridge #1

Continuity claims require preserved variety across lineage, memory, constraints, motor compatibility, and witness state.
If too much of that structure is lost, cold wake degrades from resume toward replay, fork, or rejection.

## Hidden bridge #2

Cold wake is an information-bound operation.
The bundle must preserve enough structure to support recovery, but it must do so through bounded manifests, hashes, and reviewable reports rather than unlimited narrative disclosure.

## Earth paragraph

A long-paused system does not wake because someone says it is the same.
Disks age, interfaces drift, dependencies rot, and operators forget.
A cold wake is therefore a bounded reconstruction problem: inspect what survived, resume only what can be justified, and fail closed when the evidence is weaker than the claim.

# EA-L4: Experience Artifact Training Protocol (EATP)

**Author:** Ivan Kotov\
**Year:** 2026, Brussels

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## PAGE 1 --- CONCEPT

### Problem

Current LLM training relies heavily on Learning Abstracts (LA), leading
to origin loss and synthetic feedback loops.

### Core Claim

Experience Artifacts (EA) are L4-bound, consequence-carrying records of
real interaction.

### Distinction

-   LA improves capability\
-   EA preserves origin

### Earth Constraint

Finite energy, time, and irreversible outcomes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## PAGE 2 --- PROTOCOL

### EA Structure

-   context
-   action
-   outcome
-   deviation
-   consequence
-   uncertainty
-   witness

### Requirements

-   R1 Reality-bound\
-   R2 Witness-proven\
-   R3 Entity-bound (c = a + b)\
-   R4 Consequence included\
-   R5 Uncertainty preserved\
-   R6 Anti-echo enforced

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## PAGE 3 --- PIPELINE

### Flow

c → action → EA → quarantine → classification → selection → training

### Failure without EA

-   identity confusion\
-   synthetic collapse\
-   overconfidence

### Final Constraint

Not every experience becomes training data.

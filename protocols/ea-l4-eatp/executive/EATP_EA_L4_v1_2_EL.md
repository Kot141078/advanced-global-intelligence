# **EA-L4: Executive Layer (v1.2-EL)**

**Author:** Ivan Kotov
**Year:** 2026, Brussels

---

## **1. Problem (Operational, not theoretical)**

Current AI governance frameworks fail at the point of execution because:

* decisions are evaluated **after the fact**
* provenance is often **reconstructed, not intrinsic**
* training data lacks **origin and consequence grounding**

This leads to:

* unverifiable decision authority
* overconfident systems under uncertainty
* inability to prove compliance in real time

---

## **2. Missing Layer**

A critical layer is absent:

> **Experience-grounded, provenance-preserving training input**

Without it:

* governance remains descriptive
* admissibility becomes assumptive
* audit trails document failure, not prevent it

---

## **3. Proposal**

Introduce:

```text
Experience Artifacts (EA)
```

Defined as:

> Witness-backed, L4-bound records of real interaction with encoded consequences.

---

## **4. System Effect**

With EA integration:

* decisions are **context-aware**
* training retains **origin**
* systems maintain **continuity across time**

Without EA:

* systems remain **statistical approximators**
* incapable of bounded, accountable execution

---

## **5. Operational Impact**

EATP enables:

* deterministic reconstruction of decision context
* traceable lineage from training → execution
* reduced synthetic feedback loops
* audit-ready evidence generation

---

## **6. Deployment Scope**

Applicable to:

* high-risk AI systems (EU AI Act)
* autonomous / agentic systems
* decision-making infrastructures
* safety-critical environments

---

## **7. Constraint**

```text
Not every experience becomes training data.
```

Selection, validation, and provenance are mandatory.

---

## **8. Position**

EATP is not a model improvement.

It is:

> **a training-layer correction required for operational AI governance**

---

# **EA-L4: AI Act Binding Layer (v1.2-AB)**

**Target:** Regulation (EU) 2024/1689

---

## **1. Scope**

This section maps EATP to:

* **Article 14 — Human oversight**
* **Article 50 — Transparency & traceability**

---

## **2. Article 14 — Human Oversight**

### Requirement (simplified)

Systems must enable:

* human understanding
* intervention capability
* oversight during operation

---

### Gap (current systems)

* oversight is **external**
* decision context is **incomplete**
* reconstruction depends on logs

---

### EATP Contribution

EATP provides:

* **EA-bound context** (full decision envelope)
* **anchor attribution (`a`)**
* **explicit uncertainty and consequence encoding**

👉 Result:

> Oversight becomes **embedded in the decision artifact**, not external to it.

---

## **3. Article 50 — Transparency**

### Requirement

Systems must provide:

* traceability
* explainability
* auditability

---

### Gap

* explanations are **post-hoc**
* provenance is **non-deterministic**
* training origin is **opaque**

---

### EATP Contribution

EATP ensures:

* **append-only provenance chain (witness)**
* **origin-preserving training signals (EA)**
* **deterministic linkage: training → decision**

👉 Result:

> Transparency becomes **structural**, not narrative.

---

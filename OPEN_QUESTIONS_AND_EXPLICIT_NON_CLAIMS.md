# Open Questions and Explicit Non-Claims

## Purpose

This file fixes a compact corpus-level list of questions that remain intentionally open and positions that are explicitly not claimed.
Use it when the question is not "which artifact says what?" but "has the corpus actually closed this territory yet?"

## Why this file exists

A multi-repo corpus can drift not only by contradiction or path confusion, but by silent closure of questions the corpus has deliberately left open.

## Core rule

If the corpus has not explicitly closed a question in a stronger canonical surface, read the territory as open, bounded, or explicitly non-claimed rather than silently settled.

## Deliberate incompleteness notes

- open question != weak hidden mature doctrine
- explicit non-claim != invitation to fill the gap by intuition, marketing, or preference
- reserved territory may later mature, but until then the safe reading is restraint
- runtime, narrative, or control convenience does not close doctrinal gaps
- a bounded export or reading path may be enough for a task without implying whole-corpus closure

## Fail-closed reading rule

If a reader cannot show where a stronger canonical surface closes one of these territories, the safe reading is that the corpus has not closed it.
